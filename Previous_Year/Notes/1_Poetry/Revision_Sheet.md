# **Poetry Revision Sheet**

## 1. **The Canterbury Tales** – Geoffrey Chaucer

* **Age/Era:** Medieval Age (14th Century)
* **Type:** Frame Narrative, Social Satire, Realistic
* **Main Idea:** A group of pilgrims tell stories representing different classes of medieval society.
* **Main Characters:** The Host, The Knight, The Wife of Bath, The Pardoner, other pilgrims.

---

## 2. **Paradise Lost** – John Milton

* **Age/Era:** Puritan Age (17th Century)
* **Type:** Epic, Religious, Serious
* **Main Idea:** The fall of Satan and the disobedience of Adam and Eve leading to mankind’s fall.
* **Main Characters:** Satan, Adam, Eve, God, Son of God.

---

## 3. **The Rape of the Lock** – Alexander Pope

* **Age/Era:** Neoclassical Age (18th Century)
* **Type:** Mock-Epic, Satire, Humorous
* **Main Idea:** Satire on aristocratic society over a trivial incident (cutting of a lock of hair).
* **Main Characters:** Belinda, The Baron, Ariel.

---

## 4. **Ode: Intimations of Immortality** – William Wordsworth

* **Age/Era:** Romantic Age (19th Century)
* **Type:** Ode, Philosophical, Serious
* **Main Idea:** Childhood is spiritually divine; adulthood loses that heavenly vision.
* **Main Focus:** The Poet, Childhood, Nature.

---

## 5. **Tintern Abbey** – William Wordsworth

* **Age/Era:** Romantic Age
* **Type:** Lyric Poem, Meditative, Philosophical
* **Main Idea:** Nature shapes the poet’s moral and spiritual growth.
* **Main Characters:** The Poet, Dorothy.

---

## 6. **Ulysses** – Alfred Lord Tennyson

* **Age/Era:** Victorian Age (19th Century)
* **Type:** Dramatic Monologue, Philosophical, Serious
* **Main Idea:** A hero’s restless desire for adventure and knowledge even in old age.
* **Main Characters:** Ulysses, Telemachus.

---

## 7. **The Lotos-Eaters** – Alfred Lord Tennyson

* **Age/Era:** Victorian Age
* **Type:** Narrative Poem, Mythological, Melancholic
* **Main Idea:** Sailors feel tired of life and wish to escape responsibilities.
* **Main Characters:** Sailors of Odysseus.

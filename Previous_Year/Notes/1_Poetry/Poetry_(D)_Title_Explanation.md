# Detailed(D) Poem Title Explanation

## 1. The Canterbury Tales – Geoffrey Chaucer

🏰 **Medieval Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Canterbury** = Holy place and pilgrimage destination in England ⛪
* **Tales** = Stories told by pilgrims 📖

➡️ Means: *Stories told by pilgrims traveling to Canterbury*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Canterbury symbolizes **spiritual goal and religious devotion** ✨
* The journey symbolizes **human life journey** 🚶‍♂️
* Tales symbolize **different social classes and personalities**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The entire work is based on pilgrims telling stories while traveling to Canterbury.

Example:

> The Knight tells a noble tale, the Wife of Bath tells about marriage.

👉 Title reflects the **pilgrimage, storytelling, and medieval society**

---

## 2. Paradise Lost – John Milton

🔥 **Puritan Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Paradise** = Heaven / Garden of Eden 🌿
* **Lost** = Something destroyed or taken away ❌

➡️ Means: *Heaven was lost by Adam and Eve*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Paradise symbolizes **innocence and perfection** 😇
* Lost symbolizes **sin and fall of mankind** ⚠️
* Also symbolizes **loss of spiritual purity**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

Adam and Eve disobey God and are expelled from Eden.

Example:

> They lose their perfect life in paradise.

👉 Title reflects the **fall of man and loss of innocence**

---

## 3. The Rape of the Lock – Alexander Pope

🎭 **Neoclassical Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Rape** = Taking something by force (old meaning) ✂️
* **Lock** = Strand of hair 💇‍♀️

➡️ Means: *The forceful cutting of Belinda’s hair*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Lock symbolizes **beauty and pride** 👑
* Its loss symbolizes **vanity and social foolishness**
* Shows importance given to small things

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The entire poem revolves around the cutting of Belinda’s hair.

Example:

> The Baron cuts Belinda’s lock.

👉 Title reflects the **central incident and satire on society**

---

## 4. Ode to a Nightingale – John Keats

🌙 **Romantic Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Ode** = Serious emotional poem 🙏
* **Nightingale** = A bird symbolizing immortality 🐦

➡️ Means: *A poem expressing feelings about the nightingale*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Nightingale symbolizes **immortality and eternal beauty** ✨
* Human life symbolizes **suffering and mortality**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The poet listens to the nightingale and wishes to escape human suffering.

Example:

> “Thou wast not born for death, immortal Bird!”

👉 Title reflects the **contrast between immortal bird and mortal humans**

---

## 5. To Autumn – John Keats

🍂 **Romantic Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Autumn** = Season of harvest and maturity 🌾

➡️ Means: *A poem about the autumn season*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Autumn symbolizes **maturity and fulfillment**
* Also symbolizes **cycle of life**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The poem describes the beauty and richness of autumn.

Example:

> Ripening fruits and harvest scenes.

👉 Title reflects the **season and its symbolic meaning**

---

## 6. Kubla Khan – Samuel Taylor Coleridge

🌌 **Romantic Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Kubla Khan** = Mongol emperor 👑

➡️ Means: *The emperor and his magnificent palace*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Kubla Khan symbolizes **creative power and imagination**
* Palace symbolizes **human creativity**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The poem describes Kubla Khan’s magical palace at Xanadu.

Example:

> “In Xanadu did Kubla Khan…”

👉 Title reflects the **central figure and imaginative vision**

---

## 7. The Rime of the Ancient Mariner – Samuel Taylor Coleridge

🌊 **Romantic Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Rime** = Poem or story 📜
* **Ancient** = Old
* **Mariner** = Sailor 🚢

➡️ Means: *The story of an old sailor*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Mariner symbolizes **human guilt and redemption**
* Sea symbolizes **life journey**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The mariner narrates his story of sin and punishment.

Example:

> He kills the albatross and suffers curse.

👉 Title reflects the **mariner’s story and moral lesson**

---

## 8. My Last Duchess – Robert Browning

🎭 **Victorian Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **My** = Possessive pride
* **Last Duchess** = Duke’s dead wife 👩

➡️ Means: *The Duke speaking about his dead Duchess*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Duchess symbolizes **innocence and beauty**
* Duke symbolizes **power and control**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The Duke describes his last Duchess’s portrait.

Example:

> He controlled her and caused her death.

👉 Title reflects the **Duke’s possessiveness and jealousy**

---

## 9. Prospice – Robert Browning

⚔️ **Victorian Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Prospice** = Latin word meaning “Look forward” 👁️

➡️ Means: *Facing the future bravely*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Symbolizes **courage in facing death**
* Death symbolizes **challenge, not fear**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The poet bravely faces death.

Example:

> “Fear death?—to feel the fog in my throat…”

👉 Title reflects the **poet’s fearless attitude**

---

## 10. The Last Ride Together – Robert Browning

❤️ **Victorian Age**

### 📌 Meaning of the Title:

* **Last Ride** = Final journey together 🚴
* **Together** = With his beloved

➡️ Means: *Final ride of lovers together*

---

### 🎯 Symbolism:

* Ride symbolizes **life journey**
* Last ride symbolizes **acceptance and closure**

---

### 🧠 Why the title fits perfectly:

The lover asks for one last ride after rejection.

Example:

> He accepts failure but enjoys the final moment.

👉 Title reflects the **lover’s acceptance and philosophical reflection**

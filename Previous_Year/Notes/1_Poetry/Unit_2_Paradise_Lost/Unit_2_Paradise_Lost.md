# Unit 2: John Milton- Paradise Lost Book-I

## 🌌 **Themes of *Paradise Lost***

* **Disobedience and Fall of Man**
  The central theme is Adam and Eve’s disobedience to God, which leads to the Fall and the loss of Paradise.

* **Free Will and Predestination**
  Milton emphasizes that humans and angels are given free will; the Fall happens by choice, not by fate.

* **Good vs Evil**
  The epic presents a moral conflict between God and Satan, showing how evil arises from pride and rebellion.

* **Redemption and Hope**
  Though mankind falls, God promises salvation through Christ, giving hope for future redemption.

---

## 🌀 **Epic Similes in *Paradise Lost*** (4 Bullet Points)

* **Satan compared to Leviathan**
  Satan’s huge body is compared to the sea-monster Leviathan, emphasizing his gigantic and terrifying size.

* **Fallen angels compared to autumn leaves**
  The fallen angels lying in Hell are likened to leaves scattered in autumn, showing their vast number and defeat.

* **Satan’s shield compared to the moon**
  Satan’s shield is compared to the moon seen through a telescope, highlighting epic scale and grandeur.

* **Satan compared to a tower or mountain**
  Satan’s height is compared to a tall tower or mountain, reinforcing his heroic yet terrifying appearance.

## 🌌 **Characters in *Paradise Lost***

**Main qualities · Appearance · What they represent**

---

## 1️⃣ **God**

* **Main qualities:** Omnipotent, just, merciful, all-knowing
* **Appearance:** Pure light and glory; never described physically
* **Represents:** Absolute **divine authority**, justice, and order

---

## 2️⃣ **Son of God (Christ)**

* **Main qualities:** Obedient, loving, self-sacrificing, heroic
* **Appearance:** Radiant, powerful, glorious
* **Represents:** **Redemption**, mercy, and true heroism

---

## 3️⃣ **Satan**

* **Main qualities:** Proud, ambitious, eloquent, rebellious
* **Appearance:** Gigantic, majestic yet dark; later reduced to a serpent
* **Represents:** **Evil born from pride**, misuse of free will

---

## 4️⃣ **Adam**

* **Main qualities:** Rational, loving, dignified, responsible
* **Appearance:** Strong, noble, masculine beauty
* **Represents:** **Human reason**, mankind, leadership

---

## 5️⃣ **Eve**

* **Main qualities:** Curious, emotional, beautiful, affectionate
* **Appearance:** Extremely beautiful, graceful, delicate
* **Represents:** **Human emotion**, love, temptation

---

## 6️⃣ **Beelzebub**

* **Main qualities:** Clever, persuasive, strategic
* **Appearance:** Fallen angel, less majestic than Satan
* **Represents:** **Political intelligence** and cunning evil

---

## 7️⃣ **Raphael**

* **Main qualities:** Wise, patient, loyal
* **Appearance:** Glorious angelic form
* **Represents:** **Divine guidance** and heavenly wisdom

---

## 8️⃣ **Michael**

* **Main qualities:** Brave, stern, authoritative
* **Appearance:** Warrior angel with armour
* **Represents:** **Divine justice** and future prophecy

---

## 9️⃣ **Abdiel**

* **Main qualities:** Faithful, courageous, obedient
* **Appearance:** Angelic warrior
* **Represents:** **Moral integrity** and loyalty to God

---

## 🔟 **Sin**

* **Main qualities:** Monstrous, destructive
* **Appearance:** Half-woman, half-serpent
* **Represents:** **Consequences of Satan’s rebellion**

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ **Death**

* **Main qualities:** Violent, terrifying, merciless
* **Appearance:** Shadowy, formless horror
* **Represents:** **Punishment of sin** and mortality

# ✨ **5 Most Important Quotes from *Paradise Lost***

(with meaning & significance)

---

### 1️⃣ **“Of Man’s first disobedience, and the fruit

Of that forbidden tree…”**

📌 **Significance:**

* Opening lines
* Introduces **theme of Fall of Man**
* Biblical epic tone

---

### 2️⃣ **“Better to reign in Hell, than serve in Heaven.”** (Satan)

📌 **Significance:**

* Shows Satan’s **pride and ambition**
* Explains why he rebels
* Key line for Satan’s character

---

### 3️⃣ **“The mind is its own place, and in itself

Can make a Heaven of Hell, a Hell of Heaven.”** (Satan)

📌 **Significance:**

* Psychological depth
* Shows how **attitude shapes reality**
* Often quoted in exams

---

### 4️⃣ **“Freely they stood who stood, and fell who fell.”** (God)

📌 **Significance:**

* Explains **free will**
* Justifies God’s justice
* Major theological statement

---

### 5️⃣ **“They hand in hand with wandering steps and slow,

Through Eden took their solitary way.”**

📌 **Significance:**

* Ending of the epic
* Shows **loss, sorrow, but human unity**
* Tragic yet hopeful tone

---

# 🖋️ **Syntax / Style of *Paradise Lost*** (for exams)

* Written in **blank verse** (unrhymed iambic pentameter)
* **Long, complex sentences** influenced by Latin syntax
* Heavy use of **inversions** (verb before subject)
* Elevated, formal, epic diction

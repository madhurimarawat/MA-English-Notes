# 📚 English Literature Syllabus : Smart Summary + Analysis Guide

---

## 🔑 First: What does **D** and **ND** actually mean?

### 📌 **D = Detailed**

👉 You must study:

* Themes
* Characters
* Style
* Important lines
* Background/context
  ✅ **Long answers (10:15 marks)** can come directly.

### 📌 **ND = Non-Detailed**

👉 You must study:

* Central idea
* Key themes
* Basic explanation
* A few quotes
  ❌ No deep character or full critical analysis expected.

✨ **IMPORTANT TRUTH**
Both **D and ND questions CAN be asked**, but:

* **D → long analytical questions**
* **ND → short notes / brief essays**

So yes — **read both**, but **depth changes**.

# 🧩 UNIT-WISE SUMMARY + DIFFICULTY LEVELS

---

## 🟣 UNIT-I (Classical & Epic Foundations)

### 🧔 Geoffrey Chaucer

📖 *Prologue to The Canterbury Tales* — **D**

Chaucer presents a vivid **gallery of medieval society**, using humor, satire, and irony. Each pilgrim is introduced with a **detailed character sketch**, reflecting social class, occupation, and personality. Themes include **human folly, social hierarchy, and morality**, with language blending English and French to showcase cultural sophistication. Key figures like the **Knight** embody ideal virtues, while the **Prioress** reveals social manners and affectation.

✨ Snapshot:

* Vivid portraits of medieval society
* Realistic characters
* Satire + humor

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Social types, humour
* 🟡 Moderate: Character sketches
* 🔴 Difficult: Chaucer’s realism & irony

---

### 🧚 Edmund Spenser

📖 *The Faerie Queene* (Book I) — **ND**

Spenser’s allegorical epic celebrates **virtue through the adventures of knights**. Each knight represents a moral quality, such as Holiness or Temperance, blending **myth, morality, and Protestant values**. The narrative is symbolic rather than realistic, emphasizing **moral instruction over plot**, making it easier to focus on **themes and allegorical meaning** for short answers.

✨ Snapshot:

* Allegory of virtue
* Moral & religious symbolism

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Story & virtue
* 🟡 Moderate: Allegory meaning
* 🔴 Difficult: Symbol layers (optional)

---

## 🟣 UNIT-II (Renaissance to Metaphysical)

### 🎭 William Shakespeare

📖 *Sonnets 18, 26, 54, 60, 116* — **ND**

Shakespeare explores **love, beauty, time, and immortality** in these sonnets. Central themes include the **eternal power of poetry, fidelity, and human emotion**. Poetic devices like metaphor, imagery, and rhyme enhance meaning, but for ND questions, **understanding the theme and a few key lines** is sufficient.

✨ Snapshot:

* Love, time, beauty, immortality

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Theme of love
* 🟡 Moderate: Poetic devices
* 🔴 Difficult: Line-by-line meaning

---

### 🧠 John Donne

📖 *Death Be Not Proud*, *Valediction*, *Ecstasy*, *Canonization* — **ND**

Donne’s metaphysical poetry is characterized by **intellectual conceits, wit, and complex argumentation**. Themes center on **love, spirituality, mortality, and human intellect**. ND questions usually require grasping **the main idea and clever comparison or conceit** rather than full line-by-line analysis.

✨ Snapshot:

* Metaphysical conceits
* Love + intellect

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Central idea
* 🟡 Moderate: Conceits
* 🔴 Difficult: Philosophical tone

---

### 🌌 John Milton

📖 *Paradise Lost* (Book I) — **D**

Milton’s epic narrates the **Fall of Man and Satan’s rebellion**, blending theology with poetry. Key themes include **pride, free will, obedience, and heroism**, with Satan often portrayed as a complex anti-hero. Students should focus on **epic style, character motivations, and theological ideas** for detailed answers.

✨ Snapshot:

* Satan as epic hero?
* Theme of rebellion & pride

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Story outline
* 🟡 Moderate: Satan’s character
* 🔴 Difficult: Epic style & theology

### 🌌 John Milton : *Samson Agonistes* : **D**

A tragic drama exploring **fate, human weakness, divine justice, and revenge**. Samson embodies strength and moral struggle, while the chorus reflects on **human vulnerability and divine plan**. For exams, pay attention to **context, character conflict, and philosophical reflections**.

## 🟣 UNIT-III (Neo-Classical & Elegy)

### ✒️ John Dryden

📖 *Absalom and Achitophel* — **ND**

Dryden’s political satire uses **biblical allegory** to comment on contemporary English politics. Themes include **loyalty, rebellion, and political intrigue**, with clever use of wit. ND focus: understand **the satire, allegorical names, and moral point**.

✨ Snapshot:

* Political satire
* Biblical allegory

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Satirical tone
* 🟡 Moderate: Political references
* 🔴 Difficult: Context history

---

### 🎩 Alexander Pope

📖 *The Rape of the Lock* — **D**

A **mock-epic poem** satirizing aristocratic society, vanity, and social pretensions. Uses **heroic couplets, classical allusions, and irony**. For D questions, note the **plot, characters, social commentary, and satire techniques**.

✨ Snapshot:

* Mock-epic
* Satire on high society

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Story
* 🟡 Moderate: Mock-epic style
* 🔴 Difficult: Irony & classical parallels

---

### 🌾 Thomas Gray

📖 *Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard* — **ND**

Reflects on **death, mortality, and the lives of common people**. Themes include **memory, loss, and pastoral reflection**. Focus on **mood, imagery, and social commentary** for ND-level answers.

✨ Snapshot:

* Death & common man
* Melancholy beauty

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Theme
* 🟡 Moderate: Mood
* 🔴 Difficult: Poetic imagery

---

## 🟣 UNIT-IV (Romantic Poetry)

### 🌿 William Wordsworth

📖 *Tintern Abbey*, *Immortality Ode* — **ND**

Wordsworth emphasizes **nature, memory, and spiritual growth**. Humans are inspired by **childhood innocence, natural beauty, and moral reflection**. ND-level study: themes, imagery, and Wordsworth’s philosophy of nature.

✨ Snapshot:

* Nature + memory
* Spiritual growth

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Love for nature
* 🟡 Moderate: Childhood philosophy
* 🔴 Difficult: Pantheism ideas

---

### 🔥 P.B. Shelley

📖 *Ode to the West Wind*, *Adonais* — **ND**

Shelley focuses on **revolution, renewal, death, and immortality**. Imagery of wind and nature symbolizes **change and creative energy**. ND questions: understand **themes, symbolism, and key lines**.

✨ Snapshot:

* Revolution
* Death & rebirth

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Emotion
* 🟡 Moderate: Symbolism
* 🔴 Difficult: Myth references

---

### 🌙 John Keats

📖 *Ode to a Nightingale*, *Ode to Autumn* — **ND**

Keats celebrates **beauty, art, and mortality**. Themes: human suffering vs. poetic transcendence. ND focus: **mood, imagery, and sensory beauty**.

✨ Snapshot:

* Beauty, art, mortality

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Sensory beauty
* 🟡 Moderate: Escape vs reality
* 🔴 Difficult: Negative capability

📖 *Kubla Khan*, *Ancient Mariner* — **ALL D**

Coleridge explores **supernatural, imagination, and guilt**. *Kubla Khan*: exotic visions, *Ancient Mariner*: moral and psychological struggle. D-level study: **story, symbolism, psychological meaning, and interpretation**.

✨ Snapshot:

* Supernatural
* Imagination & guilt

📊 Difficulty:

* 🟢 Easy: Story

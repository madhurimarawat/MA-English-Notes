# Unit 1: Geoffrey Chaucer- Prologue to the Canterbury Tales

## 🌿 Themes in *The Prologue to The Canterbury Tales*

* **Social diversity and realism:** The Prologue presents a realistic cross-section of medieval English society, bringing together people from different social, professional, and moral backgrounds.

* **Ecclesiastical corruption:** Chaucer exposes the moral decay, hypocrisy, and materialism of ecclesiastical figures, highlighting the gap between religious ideals and actual practice.

* **Appearance vs. reality:** Many pilgrims appear virtuous outwardly but are morally flawed beneath the surface, a contrast Chaucer reveals through ironic description.

* **Human nature and individualism:** The Prologue explores varied human traits such as greed, piety, pride, and generosity, showing people as complex individuals rather than moral types.

* **Satire and gentle irony:** Chaucer employs humour and mild satire to criticise social and ecclesiastical institutions without open condemnation, making the critique subtle yet effective.

## Character's Description

## 🧑‍ knight & clergy

### **The Knight**

* Plain, stained tunic
* Noble, brave, honourable
* **Chivalry**

### **The Squire** (Knight Son)

* Short, fashionable gown
* Youthful, romantic, courtly
* **Love & chivalry**

### **The Yeoman**

* Green coat and hood
* Skilled woodsman
* **Archery**

### **The Prioress (Madame Eglantine)**

* Neat cloak, coral beads
* Affected manners, sentimental
* **French (Stratford, not Paris)**

### **The Monk**

* Rich fur-lined robes
* Pleasure-loving, worldly
* **Anti-ascetic**

### **The Friar**

* Fine clothes, lisping speech
* Corrupt, jovial
* **Church hypocrisy**

## Important Quotes

### 1️⃣ Opening Spring Lines (MOST IMPORTANT)

> **“Whan that Aprill with his shoures soote
> The droghte of March hath perced to the roote …”**

📌 **Why important:**

* Famous opening of *The Canterbury Tales*
* Shows **spring, renewal, rebirth**, and the urge to travel
* Sets the **pilgrimage frame** of the poem

📝 **Use in exams for:** Introduction, theme of pilgrimage, Chaucer’s realism

---

### 2️⃣ Knight’s Description (Ideal Society)

> **“He was a verray, parfit gentil knight.”**

📌 **Why important:**

* Defines the **ideal medieval knight**
* Represents **chivalry, honour, truth, nobility**
* Chaucer’s **moral benchmark** for other characters

📝 **Use in exams for:** Character sketch of Knight, medieval ideals

---

### 3️⃣ Wife of Bath (Female Voice & Experience)

> **“Experience, though noon auctoritee
> Were in this world, is right ynogh for me.”**

📌 **Why important:**

* Strong assertion of **experience over bookish authority**
* Feminist tone (very advanced for Chaucer’s time)
* Reveals Wife of Bath’s **bold, independent personality**

📝 **Use in exams for:** Feminism, women characters, realism

---

### 4️⃣ Pardoner (Hypocrisy of the Church)

> **“Radix malorum est cupiditas.”
> (The root of all evil is greed.)**

📌 **Why important:**

* Irony: Pardoner preaches against greed but **is greedy himself**
* Exposes **corruption of the medieval Church**
* Example of Chaucer’s **satire**

📝 **Use in exams for:** Satire, Church corruption, irony

# Poets and Era and Creations Explanation

# 🟡 ALEXANDER POPE

## ✍️ Poet Details

* **Age:** Neo-Classical Age
* **Period:** 18th century (Age of Reason)

### 🏷 Famous Titles of Pope (given by critics/historians)

* **“Poet of the Age of Reason”** – Samuel Johnson
* **“Master of the Heroic Couplet”** – Contemporary critics
* **“Satirist of English Society”** – Literary historians

👉 Remember: **Pope = satire, reason, balance, mockery**

---

## 📘 *The Rape of the Lock*

### 🕰 Age of Poem

* Neo-Classical Age

### 🎯 Central Idea

> A trivial social incident treated like an epic to mock aristocratic vanity.

### 🌱 Main Themes

* Vanity of upper-class society
* Mock-heroism
* Satire of social manners
* Gender roles
* Artificial values

### 🔑 Keywords

* Mock-epic
* Satire
* Heroic couplet
* Sylphs
* Social criticism

### 📌 Why Important

👉 **Perfect example of mock-epic poetry** in English literature.

---

# 🟢 WILLIAM WORDSWORTH

## ✍️ Poet Details

* **Age:** Romantic Age
* **Period:** Late 18th – early 19th century

### 🏷 Famous Titles of Wordsworth (given by critics/historians)

* **“High Priest of Nature”** – Matthew Arnold
* **“Poet of Nature and Childhood”** – Coleridge
* **“Lake Poet”** – Contemporary literary circles
* **“Poet of Spiritual Democracy”** – Critic phrase
* **Position:** Poet Laureate of England (1843–1850)

👉 Remember: **Wordsworth = nature, memory, childhood, soul**

---

## 📜 1. *Ode: Intimations of Immortality*

### 🕰 Age of Poem

* Romantic Age (later phase)

### 🎯 Central Idea

> The human soul is immortal and existed before birth.

### 🌱 Main Themes

* Childhood innocence
* Loss of divine vision
* Immortality of soul
* Memory
* Spiritual growth

### 🔑 Keywords

* Pre-existence of soul
* Immortality
* Childhood vision
* Loss and compensation
* Philosophical Romanticism

### 📌 Why Important

👉 Wordsworth’s **most philosophical poem**.

---

## 📜 2. *Tintern Abbey*

### 🕰 Age of Poem

* Romantic Age

### 🎯 Central Idea

> Nature shapes human mind, morals, and emotions over time.

### 🌱 Main Themes

* Nature as teacher
* Memory
* Growth of mind
* Nature & humanity
* Pantheism

### 🔑 Keywords

* Nature worship
* Memory
* Spiritual healing
* Pantheism
* Moral influence of nature

### 📌 Why Important

👉 Core text for **Wordsworth’s philosophy of nature**.

---

# 🟣 ALFRED LORD TENNYSON

## ✍️ Poet Details

* **Age:** Victorian Age
* **Period:** 19th century
* **Position:** Poet Laureate of England (1850–1892)

### 🏷 Famous Titles of Tennyson (given by critics/historians)

* **“Representative Poet of the Victorian Age”** – Matthew Arnold
* **“Poet of Melancholy and Moral Struggle”** – Edward Dowden
* **“Voice of the Victorian Crisis of Faith”** – A.C. Bradley

👉 Remember: **Tennyson = Victorian doubt, grief, faith vs science**

---

## 📜 1. *The Lotos-Eaters*

### 🕰 Age of Poem

* Victorian Age (early phase)

### 🎯 Central Idea

> Escape from duty and responsibility through dreamy inactivity.

### 🌱 Main Themes

* Weariness of life
* Desire for rest & escape
* Conflict between duty and pleasure
* Temptation of passivity

### 🔑 Keywords

* Lethargy
* Indolence
* Dream-like state
* Escape
* Anti-heroism

### 📌 Why Important

👉 Shows **Victorian anxiety** about work, duty, and moral responsibility.

---

## 📜 2. *Ulysses*

### 🕰 Age of Poem

* Victorian Age

### 🎯 Central Idea

> The heroic spirit that refuses to surrender to age, time, or death.

### 🌱 Main Themes

* Struggle against stagnation
* Heroism
* Quest for knowledge
* Action vs inaction
* Old age and purpose

### 🔑 Keywords

* Heroic individualism
* Restless spirit
* Quest
* Perseverance
* Willpower

### 📌 Famous Line (quote-ready)

> “To strive, to seek, to find, and not to yield.”

### 📌 Why Important

👉 Symbol of **Victorian optimism + moral courage**.

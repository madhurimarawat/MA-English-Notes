# 📘 POETRY 1: THE CANTERBURY TALES (Chaucer)

### 🚨 IF YOU SEE:

• Middle English spelling (Ful, hir, bigan, ycleped, swich)
• Description of pilgrims
• Knight / Prioress / Monk / Friar
• Church corruption (but gently humorous tone)
• Words like: chivalrie, curteisie, trouthe
• Physical appearance described in detail
• Irony in praising a character

➡️ ✅ IT IS **CANTERBURY TALES**

---

### 🔎 Apply to Your Examples:

(a) “Ful wel she sung…”
👉 Middle English spelling
👉 Description of Prioress
➡️ ✅ CANTERBURY TALES

(b) “He loved chivalrie, Trouthe and honour…”
👉 Knight praised
👉 Medieval values
➡️ ✅ CANTERBURY TALES

---

### 💡 QUICK MEMORY TRICK:

If it sounds OLD + MEDIEVAL + PILGRIMS
= CHAUCER

• Light but sarcastic tone

➡️ ✅ IT IS **RAPE OF THE LOCK**

---

### 🔎 Apply to Your Examples:

👉 Satire on female vanity
➡️ ✅ RAPE OF THE LOCK

(f) “Oh thoughtless mortals! ever blind to fate…”
👉 Mock-epic tone
👉 Overdramatic for small issue
➡️ ✅ RAPE OF THE LOCK

# Poem Stanza Identification

### (a) “Ful wel she sung the service divine…”

✅ **Poem**: *The Canterbury Tales*
✅ **Character**: The Prioress
✅ **Context**: From the General Prologue, describing her manners and behavior.

🔎 **Explanation**:
Chaucer describes the Prioress as refined and elegant. She sings well and speaks French in a fashionable way. However, the tone is slightly ironic — Chaucer subtly suggests she is more concerned with courtly manners than spiritual devotion.

🎯 Theme: Appearance vs reality / Satire of clergy

---

### (b) “That fro the tyme that he first bigan…”

✅ **Poem**: *The Canterbury Tales*
✅ **Character**: The Knight
✅ **Context**: Description of the ideal medieval knight.

🔎 **Explanation**:
The Knight is presented as the perfect embodiment of chivalry — truth, honor, courtesy, and bravery. He represents medieval ideals of knighthood.

🎯 Theme: Chivalry / Feudal values

---

### (c) “The broad circumference, hung on his shoulders…”

✅ **Poem**: *Paradise Lost*
✅ **Character**: Satan
✅ **Context**: Description of Satan’s shield compared to the moon.

🔎 **Explanation**:
Milton uses epic simile to describe Satan’s massive shield like the moon viewed through Galileo’s telescope. This elevates Satan to epic grandeur.

🎯 Theme: Epic scale / Heroic imagery

---

### (d) “What though the field be lost? All is not lost…”

✅ **Poem**: *Paradise Lost*
✅ **Character**: Satan
✅ **Context**: After being cast out of Heaven.

🔎 **Explanation**:
Satan refuses to submit despite defeat. He emphasizes “unconquerable will.” This speech shows pride and rebellion.

🎯 Theme: Pride / Defiance / Free will

---

### (e) “Think not, when woman’s transient breath is fled…”

✅ **Poem**: *The Rape of the Lock*
✅ **Context**: Mock-epic tone about feminine vanity.

🔎 **Explanation**:
Pope humorously says that even after death, a woman’s vanity continues. It satirizes aristocratic superficiality.

🎯 Theme: Satire of society / Vanity

---

### (f) “Oh thoughtless mortals! ever blind to fate…”

✅ **Poem**: *The Rape of the Lock*
✅ **Context**: Mock-epic moral warning.

🔎 **Explanation**:
The poet mocks how humans exaggerate small events (like the cutting of a lock of hair). The tone is ironically grand.

🎯 Theme: Triviality vs epic seriousness

---

### (g) “My heart aches, and a drowsy numbness pains…”

✅ **Poem**: *Ode to a Nightingale* – John Keats

🔎 **Context**: Opening lines of the poem.

🔎 **Explanation**:
The speaker feels emotional pain and numbness, almost like being drugged. It expresses melancholy and desire to escape reality.

🎯 Theme: Mortality / Escape / Romantic melancholy

---

### (h) “A heart – how shall I say? – too soon made glad…”

✅ **Poem**: *My Last Duchess* – Robert Browning
✅ **Speaker**: The Duke

🔎 **Context**: Duke describing his dead wife.

🔎 **Explanation**:
The Duke criticizes the Duchess for being too easily pleased. This reveals his jealousy and possessiveness.

🎯 Theme: Power / Patriarchy / Control

---

### (i) “Fear death? – to feel the fog in my throat…”

✅ **Poem**: *Prospice* – Robert Browning

🔎 **Context**: The speaker challenges death boldly.

🔎 **Explanation**:
The speaker does not fear death but confronts it bravely, seeing it as a struggle to be faced.

🎯 Theme: Courage / Confronting death

---

### (j) “My mistress bent that brow of hers…”

✅ **Poem**: *The Last Ride Together* – Robert Browning

🔎 **Context**: Lover speaking to his beloved.

🔎 **Explanation**:
The speaker observes his mistress’ pride and emotional restraint. The poem reflects romantic hope despite rejection.

🎯 Theme: Love / Acceptance / Optimism

---

# 🔥 QUICK IDENTIFICATION FLOWCHART FOR POETRY

IF Middle English spellings (Ful, hir, bigan) → Canterbury Tales

IF Grand epic language + Satan + cosmic scale → Paradise Lost

IF Mock-heroic tone + vanity + fashionable ladies → Rape of the Lock

IF Melancholy + nightingale + numbness → Ode to a Nightingale

IF Autumn imagery + harvest → To Autumn

IF Dreamlike palace + exotic imagery → Kubla Khan

IF Sea + albatross + curse → Rime of the Ancient Mariner

IF Jealous Duke speaking → My Last Duchess

IF Fearless about death → Prospice

IF Romantic monologue about failed love → Last Ride Together

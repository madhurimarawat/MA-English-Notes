## 📖 What is M.A. English?

**M.A. English** is an advanced postgraduate course that studies **English literature across centuries**, focusing on **poetry, prose, fiction, drama**, and the **history of the English language**.
It develops **critical thinking, textual analysis, structured academic writing**, and literary interpretation 🧠✨

📚 The programme is **divided into two academic years**:

* **M.A. Previous Year** – consists of **5 core subjects**
* **M.A. Final Year** – also consists of **5 subjects**, focusing on **advanced texts, criticism, and deeper literary analysis**

📘 In **both Previous and Final years**:

* **Papers I to IV** are **compulsory**
* **Paper V** is **optional**, where **two subjects are offered**, and **one must be chosen**
* This optional paper structure applies **in both Previous and Final years**
  
Together, both years offer a **comprehensive and progressive understanding of English literature and language** 🌿📖

<a id="onedesc"></a>

## 🧩 One-Line Description of Each Subject

* **📘 Poetry:** Study of poetic forms, imagery, symbolism, emotions, and movements from Chaucer to modern poets.
* * **🎭 Drama:** Plays focusing on conflict, characterisation, dialogue, and stagecraft.
* **📗 Prose:** Essays and discursive writings focusing on ideas, arguments, philosophy, and style.
* **📕 Fiction:** Novels and short stories analyzing plot, character, society, and psychology.
* **🕰️ History of English Language:** Development of English from Old English to Modern English:sounds, grammar, and vocabulary.

## 🧠 How the Syllabus Is Structured

* Each paper has **5–6 Units**
* Each unit contains **2–4 writers or major texts**
* In the exam, **ONE question comes from EACH unit**
* **Internal choice (OR)** is always given ✅

<a id="unit1"></a>

## ⭐ Unit-I : Common Passage Unit (MOST IMPORTANT)

### 📌 What is Unit-I?

🚨 **Unit-I is NOT a real syllabus unit.**

It is a **COMMON PASSAGE UNIT** containing **extracts / stanzas from the ENTIRE syllabus**, not from one unit.

✔️ Covers **ALL writers + ALL periods**
✔️ Same logic for **Poetry, Drama, Prose, Fiction**

---

### ✍️ Question Pattern (Unit-I)

**Question 1:**
👉 *“Explain with reference to the context any FIVE of the following passages.”*

* 8–10 passages given
* Attempt **ANY FIVE**
* Each answer includes:

  * Author & text
  * Context
  * Explanation
  * Literary significance

📖 Tests **recognition + overall reading**

✅ So yes:

> **Unit-I contains stanzas/passages from all contents of the syllabus.**

---

<a id="units"></a>

## 🧱 Unit-II to Unit-VI : Actual Syllabus Units

From **Unit-II onwards**, the **real syllabus begins**.

* Each unit focuses on:

  * A **period**
  * A **movement**
  * Or **specific writers**

### 📝 Question Pattern

* One long answer per unit
* Internal choice (OR)
* Length: ~400 words
* Marks: **16 each**

You attempt **ONE question from EACH unit**.

<a id="history"></a>

## 🕰️ History of English Language : Exam Pattern

* Total **5 Units**
* ❌ No passage-based questions
* ✅ **Direct theory questions only**
* 🔁 **Internal choice (OR) is given**
* 🧮 Each question = **20 marks**
* 📌 One question from each unit (compulsory)

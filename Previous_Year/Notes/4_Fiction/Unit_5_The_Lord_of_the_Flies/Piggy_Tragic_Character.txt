Piggy Tragic Character:

Piggy is the most tragic character in **Lord of the Flies** because he represents intelligence, reason, and civilization in a world that increasingly values violence and power. From the beginning, Piggy is physically weak, asthmatic, and overweight, which makes him an easy target for ridicule and exclusion. 

Despite this, he is the most rational thinker on the island—he suggests using the conch to call meetings, emphasizes the importance of rules, and understands the need for the signal fire. However, the other boys ignore and mock him because they respect physical strength more than intellectual ability. Piggy’s glasses, which symbolize knowledge and scientific progress, are stolen and used for destructive purposes, showing how reason is overpowered by savagery. 

His death is especially tragic because he is killed suddenly and senselessly by Roger, and at the same moment, the conch shell shatters, symbolizing the complete destruction of order and civilization. Piggy dies not because he is wrong, but because he lives in a society that no longer values truth, logic, or humanity. His fate reveals Golding’s powerful message that intelligence and morality alone cannot survive without the protection of civilization, and in a savage world, the voice of reason is easily silenced.

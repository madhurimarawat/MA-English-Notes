# **Gulliver’s Travels – Timeline with Key Points**

---

## **1️⃣ Lilliput (Tiny People)**

* **Event:** Gulliver shipwrecks and lands in Lilliput → he is gigantic compared to the tiny people.
* **Key Episodes:**

  * Tied up as a prisoner → eventually helps them in a war.
  * Receives honors, then falls out of favor.
* **Symbolism / Main Point:**

  * Lilliputians = petty humans
  * Small wars over trivial things (high heels vs low heels) = political absurdity
* **Main Satire:**

  * Vanity, pride, political pettiness, human foolishness

---

## **2️⃣ Brobdingnag (Giants)**

* **Event:** Gulliver is now tiny in a land of giants.
* **Key Episodes:**

  * Lives in a giant household, is treated as a curiosity.
  * Shows king Europeans’ weapons → king horrified by human cruelty.
* **Symbolism / Main Point:**

  * Giants = moral and physical contrast → humans are small, weak, cruel
  * Shows human pride and war-making as ridiculous
* **Main Satire:**

  * Criticism of European politics, cruelty, greed, and arrogance

---

## **3️⃣ Laputa (Floating Island)**

* **Event:** Gulliver visits a floating island of scholars and scientists.
* **Key Episodes:**

  * Residents obsessed with abstract knowledge
  * Cannot manage practical life → food and society suffer
* **Symbolism / Main Point:**

  * Laputans = obsession with useless knowledge
  * Satire of scientific arrogance and intellectual disconnect
* **Main Satire:**

  * Ridicules impractical science, obsession with theories, and human ignorance

---

## **4️⃣ Glubbdubdrib / Luggnagg / Balnibarbi (Optional, brief)**

* **Glubbdubdrib:** Land of sorcerers → Gulliver talks to the dead → shows historical truths vs myths
* **Luggnagg:** Immortals who suffer → warns against endless life
* **Balnibarbi:** Society ruined by impractical science → same satire as Laputa

---

## **5️⃣ Houyhnhnms (Wise Horses)**

* **Event:** Gulliver encounters rational horses (Houyhnhnms) and savage humans (Yahoos).
* **Key Episodes:**

  * Gulliver admires Houyhnhnms’ honesty, simplicity, and reason
  * Yahoos = humans → savage, greedy, lustful
* **Symbolism / Main Point:**

  * Houyhnhnms = ideal reason and morality
  * Yahoos = humans’ base instincts
* **Main Satire:**

  * Critique of human nature → greed, lust, violence, irrationality
  * Shows humans are morally inferior to animals

---

## **6️⃣ Return Home**

* **Event:** Gulliver returns to England
* **Key Episode:**

  * He cannot bear human company, admires Houyhnhnms → satirical pessimism about humanity
* **Symbolism / Main Point:**

  * Shows Swift’s extreme criticism of human society
* **Main Satire:**

  * Human pride, corruption, hypocrisy, moral decay

---

## Important Quotes

### 1. **“I cannot but conclude the bulk of your natives to be the most pernicious race of little odious vermin that nature ever suffered to crawl upon the surface of the earth.”**

* **Speaker:** King of Brobdingnag (after Gulliver narrated about humans)
* **Meaning:** Criticizes european wars and politics after Gulliver narrates about humans.

### 2. **“We are almost always wiser in other people's affairs than in our own.”**

* **Speaker:** Lemuel Gulliver
* **Meaning:** Swift points out human hypocrisy and tendency to judge others better than oneself—a reflection on self-awareness and moral blindness.

### 3. **“I cannot but think the whole race of mankind, with few exceptions, are most unaccountably mad.”**

* **Speaker:** Lemuel Gulliver
* **Meaning:** After seeing the absurdity and pettiness of human behavior in places like Lilliput and Brobdingnag, Gulliver questions human rationality. Swift satirizes human foolishness.

### 4. **“A wise man should be cautious of saying what he thinks.”**

* **Speaker:** Struldbrugs (immortal beings in the land of the immortals) / Narration context
* **Meaning:** Words can be dangerous; Swift emphasizes prudence and the consequences of speaking without thought.

### 5. **“When I set my eyes upon them, I was filled with disgust.”**

* **Speaker:** Gulliver (about Yahoos in Houyhnhnm land)
* **Meaning:** Swift uses the Yahoos, filthy and base humans, to reflect humanity’s moral degradation contrasted with the virtuous Houyhnhnms.

### 6. **“Nothing is more evident than that all men desire to live happily; but if we examine how they go to work to attain it, we shall find they generally destroy it.”**

* **Speaker:** Lemuel Gulliver / Narration
* **Meaning:** Swift critiques human self-defeating behavior; people pursue happiness in ways that ironically prevent it.

### 7. **“The best books, like the best friends, are those that tell you the truth about yourself.”**

* **Speaker:** Narration (Gulliver reflecting)
* **Meaning:** Swift emphasizes learning and self-awareness through honest reflection, often via satire.

### 8. **“I began to view myself as a Yahoo.”**

* **Speaker:** Lemuel Gulliver (narrating about humans)
* **Meaning:** After traveling to the land of the Houyhnhnms (rational horses), Gulliver expresses his disgust at humanity, comparing humans to lustful base Yahoos. Swift criticizes human pride, cruelty, and corruption.

## Book III:

Book III is mainly a **satire on science, philosophers, historians, and impractical intellectuals**.

# 🌍 1️⃣ **Laputa**

## 📌 What is Laputa?

Laputa is a **floating island** in the sky. It flies above another country called Balnibarbi.

## 👥 Who lives there?

The people of Laputa:

* Are obsessed with **mathematics and music**
* Always thinking about calculations
* Cannot talk normally without using geometry terms
* Need servants called “flappers” to hit them gently so they pay attention 😅

## 🧠 Why do they need flappers?

Because they are always lost in deep thought.
A servant carries a stick with a bladder and taps their:

* Mouth → so they speak
* Ear → so they listen

## 🏛️ Political Control

The King of Laputa controls the land below (Balnibarbi) by:

* Blocking sunlight ☀️
* Throwing rocks down
* Threatening destruction

## 🎯 What is Swift criticizing?

Swift is mocking:

* Useless scientific theories
* Over-intellectual people
* Scientists who ignore practical life
* The Royal Society in England (indirectly)

Laputa shows **knowledge without common sense**.

---

# 🌍 2️⃣ **Balnibarbi**

## 📌 What is Balnibarbi?

It is the **country under Laputa**.
The floating island rules over it.

## 🌾 Condition of the country

* Poor
* Fields are damaged
* Buildings are broken
* People are suffering

Why? Because they are trying to apply strange scientific experiments.

## 🧪 The Academy of Lagado

In Balnibarbi, Gulliver visits the Academy of Lagado where scientists try foolish experiments like:

* Extracting sunlight from cucumbers 🥒
* Turning human waste back into food 🤢
* Building houses from the roof downward
* Teaching mathematics by eating wafers with formulas

All experiments fail.

## 🎯 What is Swift criticizing?

* Blind faith in experiments
* Impractical research
* Government funding useless projects

Balnibarbi represents **ruined society due to foolish reforms**.

---

# 🌍 3️⃣ **Glubbdubdrib**

## 📌 What is Glubbdubdrib?

It is called the **Island of Sorcerers** 🧙‍♂️.

## 👑 Who rules it?

A governor who has magical powers.

## 👻 Special Power

The governor can:

* Call ghosts of dead people
* Make them speak the truth

Gulliver talks to:

* Ancient kings
* Roman leaders
* Philosophers
* Historical figures

## 😲 What does Gulliver learn?

He discovers:

* History books lie
* Famous heroes were corrupt
* Great rulers were not noble

## 🎯 What is Swift criticizing?

* False history
* Corrupt politics
* Glorification of rulers
* Fake greatness

Glubbdubdrib shows that **real history is different from written history**.

---

# 🌍 4️⃣ **Luggnagg**

## 📌 What is Luggnagg?

Another island Gulliver visits after Glubbdubdrib.

## 👑 Strange Custom

People must crawl before the King.

## 🧬 The Struldbrugs

This is the most important part.

Struldbrugs are:

* People born with a red mark on their forehead
* They never die

## 😍 At first Gulliver thinks:

“Wow! Immortality is amazing!”

## 😢 Reality:

Struldbrugs:

* Grow old forever
* Become weak
* Lose memory
* Become miserable
* Are legally considered dead at age 80

They are cursed, not blessed.

## 🎯 What is Swift criticizing?

* Human desire for immortality
* Fear of death
* The illusion that long life = happiness

Swift shows that **immortality without youth is a curse**.

# 🎯 Overall Meaning of Book III

In this part of ***Gulliver’s Travels***, Jonathan Swift attacks:

* Scientists without practicality
* Politicians
* Historians
* Reformers
* Human pride
* Desire for eternal life

It is more serious and philosophical than Lilliput.

# 📘 Meanings of Names in Gulliver’s Travels

**Author:** Jonathan Swift
**Book:** Gulliver’s Travels

This file contains **accurate meanings and motives** behind all important names.

---

# 🌍 1. Gulliver — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

From **“gullible”** → easily fooled
Also related to **“gull”** → traveler, wanderer

## 🎯 Motive:

* Represents ordinary human beings
* Curious but easily influenced

## ✅ Exam Line:

Gulliver symbolizes the curious and sometimes gullible nature of humans.

---

# 🌍 2. Lilliput — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

From **“little”**

## 🎯 Motive:

* Represents small size physically
* Symbolizes small-mindedness mentally

## ✅ Exam Line:

Lilliput symbolizes petty politics and small-minded human pride.

---

# 🌍 3. Brobdingnag — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Swift invented the word, but it sounds like **big, heavy, gigantic**

## 🎯 Motive:

* Represents greatness and moral clarity
* Makes humans look small and weak

## ✅ Exam Line:

Brobdingnag symbolizes moral superiority and exposes human weakness.

---

# 🌍 4. Laputa — Meaning (Important Correction)

## 📌 Meaning:

Laputa likely comes from Spanish **“la puta”**

* **“La puta” = prostitute**
* Also used to mean **morally corrupt person**

⚠️ Important: Swift never officially confirmed this, but scholars strongly believe this was intentional satire.

## 🎯 Motive:

Laputa represents:

* Intellectual corruption
* Pride in useless knowledge
* Abuse of intellectual power

The floating island controls others but produces nothing useful.

## ✅ Exam Line:

Laputa symbolizes intellectual pride and corruption through useless knowledge.

---

# 🌍 5. Balnibarbi — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Invented word, but sounds like:

* Babble (confusion)
* Disorder

## 🎯 Motive:

Represents failed scientific reforms and confused society.

## ✅ Exam Line:

Balnibarbi symbolizes destruction caused by foolish scientific experiments.

---

# 🌍 6. Glubbdubdrib — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Invented magical-sounding word.

## 🎯 Motive:

Represents illusion and false historical glorification.

## ✅ Exam Line:

Glubbdubdrib symbolizes the difference between real and false history.

---

# 🌍 7. Luggnagg — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Sounds like burden or heaviness.

## 🎯 Motive:

Represents burden of immortality through Struldbrugs.

## ✅ Exam Line:

Luggnagg symbolizes immortality as a burden rather than a blessing.

---

# 🌍 8. Struldbrug — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Harsh invented word suggesting unpleasant condition.

## 🎯 Motive:

Represents misery of eternal life without youth.

## ✅ Exam Line:

Struldbrugs symbolize the suffering caused by immortality.

---

# 🌍 9. Yahoo — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Swift invented this word.

Later, the English language adopted "Yahoo" to mean rude or uncivilized person.

## 🎯 Motive:

Represents worst human qualities:

* Greed
* Dirtiness
* Violence
* Lack of reason

## ✅ Exam Line:

Yahoos symbolize the savage and animalistic nature of humans.

---

# 🌍 10. Houyhnhnm — Meaning

## 📌 Meaning:

Represents sound of horse neighing.

## 🎯 Motive:

Represents:

* Pure reason
* Logic
* Perfect society

## ✅ Exam Line:

Houyhnhnms symbolize ideal rational beings and perfect logical society.

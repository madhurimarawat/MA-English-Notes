# 📘 *The Pilgrim’s Progress*

by John Bunyan

## 🏷 Title Significance

“Pilgrim” = spiritual traveler 🚶‍♂️
“Progress” = gradual moral & spiritual growth

➡ The title shows life as a **journey from sin to salvation**.

## 🎭 Symbolism

* 🏙 City of Destruction → sinful world
* 🌊 Slough of Despond → depression & doubt
* 🛍 Vanity Fair → worldly temptation
* 🌉 River of Death → final test before salvation
* 🌟 Celestial City → Heaven

## 🌍 Representation

Represents **Christian spiritual life** and Puritan belief in salvation through faith.

## 💡 Meaning

Life is a moral struggle; faith + perseverance lead to redemption.

# 📘 *Robinson Crusoe*

by Daniel Defoe

## 🏷 Title Significance

Named after the hero → focus on individual experience 🧍‍♂️

Represents **individualism & colonial mindset** of 18th century.

## 💡 Meaning

Human effort + faith = survival & mastery over nature.

# 📘 *Lord of the Flies*

by William Golding

## 🏷 Title Significance (VERY IMPORTANT)

“Lord” = ruler or master 👑
“Flies” = insects that gather around dead bodies 🪰

Literal meaning → ruler of flies

Symbolic meaning → **Lord of the Flies = Devil (Satan)**

The pig’s head covered with flies represents evil.

➡ The title means **evil is the ruler of human nature**.

It shows that when civilization disappears, evil controls humans.

## 🎭 Symbolism

* 🐖 Pig’s head → evil (Lord of the Flies)
* 🪵 Conch → law and order
* 🔥 Fire → civilization

## 🌍 Representation

Human nature without society.

## 💡 Meaning

Humans naturally have evil instincts.

In **Lord of the Flies** by **William Golding**, the name *Lord of the Flies* symbolizes **inner evil 😈, moral decay 🩸, and savagery 🔥** within human beings. The title refers to the pig’s head that **Jack and his hunters** kill and mount on a stick as an offering to the “beast”; as flies swarm around the rotting head, it becomes a powerful image of corruption and darkness. When Simon confronts it, the head seems to speak, revealing that the “beast” is not an external monster but the evil inside the boys themselves. The phrase “Lord of the Flies” is a translation of **Beelzebub**, who is often identified with **Satan** (sometimes linked with Lucifer), reinforcing the idea that the true devil is the human heart. Thus, the symbol shows that when civilization collapses, hidden brutality rises to the surface.

# 🟣 UNIT–I (Early Fiction)

# Jonathan Swift – *Gulliver's Travels*

---

## 📖 Detailed Summary

The novel narrates the four voyages of Lemuel Gulliver.

In **Lilliput**, he meets tiny people and observes petty political conflicts.
In **Brobdingnag**, he becomes tiny among giants and sees human weakness clearly.
In **Laputa**, he encounters foolish scientists and intellectual absurdity.
In the land of **Houyhnhnms**, rational horses rule while brutal humans (Yahoos) represent corruption.

Through fantasy travel, Swift satirizes politics, pride, science, and human nature.

---

## 🎯 Themes

### 1️⃣ Human Folly

* Humans are selfish and corrupt.

### 2️⃣ Political Satire

* Criticism of British politics and war.

### 3️⃣ Civilization vs Savagery

* Humans behave worse than animals.

---

## 👤 Key Characters

### 🌍 Lemuel Gulliver

* Traveller and narrator.
* Represents changing human perspective.

### 🐎 Houyhnhnms

* Rational horses.
* Symbol of reason and ideal society.

### 🐒 Yahoos

* Savage human-like creatures.
* Represent human brutality.

---

* Speaker: Houyhnhnm philosophy

# 🟣 UNIT–II (18th-Century Novel)

# Samuel Richardson – *Pamela*

---

## 📖 Detailed Summary

*Pamela* is written in letter form. Pamela, a young servant girl, resists her master Mr. B’s improper advances. Despite pressure and emotional manipulation, she protects her virtue.

Eventually, Mr. B reforms and marries her. The novel ends with virtue rewarded.

---

## 🎯 Themes

### 1️⃣ Virtue Rewarded

* Morality brings social upliftment.

### 2️⃣ Class & Social Mobility

* A servant rises in status.

### 3️⃣ Female Integrity

* Strength of moral character.

---

## 👤 Characters

### 👩 Pamela

* Virtuous, strong-willed heroine.

### 👨 Mr. B

* Initially immoral, later reforms.

# Sir Walter Scott – *Ivanhoe*

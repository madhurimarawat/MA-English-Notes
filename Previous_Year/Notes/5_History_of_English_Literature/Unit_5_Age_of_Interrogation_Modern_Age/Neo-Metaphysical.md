# 🧠 Neo-Metaphysical Poetry

---

## 📖 1. Meaning of Neo-Metaphysical Poetry

**Definition:**
**Neo-Metaphysical poetry is modern poetry that revives the style, techniques, and intellectual depth of the Metaphysical poets like John Donne.**

**Easy Meaning:**
Neo-Metaphysical poetry = **modern poetry with deep thinking 🧠, philosophy ✝️, symbolism 🔗, and complex comparisons (conceits).**

**Break word meaning:**

* Neo = New
* Metaphysical = philosophical poetry

👉 So meaning = **New version of metaphysical poetry**

---

## 📅 2. Period of Neo-Metaphysical Poetry

📆 Time: **Early 20th century (1900–1950)**

It is part of **Modern Age poetry**

Poets were influenced by Metaphysical poets like John Donne

---

## 🧠 3. Main Ideas / Central Themes

Neo-Metaphysical poets focus on:

* 🧠 Intellectual thinking
* 💀 Death and mortality
* ✝️ Religion and spirituality
* ⏳ Time and eternity
* 😔 Modern life problems
* 🤯 Confusion and isolation

**Keywords:**
Philosophy • Death • Religion • Symbolism • Modern crisis • Intellect

---

## ✍️ 4. Major Neo-Metaphysical Poets (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

### 1. T. S. Eliot ⭐ (MOST IMPORTANT)

Leader of Neo-Metaphysical poetry

**Famous poems:**

* The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock
* The Waste Land
* Journey of the Magi

---

### Example with stanza:

From *The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock*

> “Let us go then, you and I,
> When the evening is spread out against the sky
> Like a patient etherised upon a table”

---

### Meaning explained:

Comparison (Conceit):

Evening sky = patient on operation table

Meaning:

* Modern life is sick 🤒
* Society is spiritually dead

**Keywords:**
Conceit • Symbolism • Modern decay

---

### 2. W. H. Auden

**Famous poem:**
September 1, 1939

Example stanza:

> “I sit in one of the dives
> On Fifty-second Street
> Uncertain and afraid”

Meaning:

* Modern man feels fear and uncertainty
* Reflects World War anxiety

Keywords:

Fear • Modern crisis • Isolation

---

### 3. Stephen Spender

Themes:

* Social injustice
* Modern society problems

Keywords:

Modern suffering • Philosophy • Society

---

### 4. Herbert Read

Themes:

* Philosophy
* Symbolism

Keywords:

Symbolism • Intellectual poetry

## 🧾 5. Main Characteristics / Features (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

### 🧠 1. Intellectual and Philosophical Poetry

Focus on thinking, not emotion

Example:

Eliot’s Prufrock thinks constantly

---

### 🔗 2. Use of Conceits (MOST IMPORTANT)

Conceit = strange comparison

Example:

Evening sky compared to patient

Meaning:
Modern life is sick

---

### 🧩 3. Symbolism

Symbols used to represent ideas

Example:

Waste Land = modern spiritual emptiness

---

### 💀 4. Death and Spiritual Crisis

Modern people lost faith in religion

---

### 😔 5. Isolation and Loneliness

Modern man feels alone

Example:

Prufrock feels socially isolated

---

### 📚 6. Complex Language

Hard to understand

Requires thinking

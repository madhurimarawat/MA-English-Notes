# ⚙️ Modern Age of English Literature (1890–1950)

The Modern Age began after the Victorian Age and was shaped by **industrialization, World War I, scientific progress, and psychological discoveries**. Literature became **experimental, realistic, pessimistic, and psychologically deep**.

---

# 🧠 MASTER FLOWCHART: Rise of Modern Literature

```id="mod1"
Victorian Age (Optimism, morality)
            ↓
Industrialization and scientific progress ⚙️
Darwin, Freud influenced thinking
            ↓
World War I (1914–1918) 💣
Death, destruction, pessimism
            ↓
Modern Age begins (1901–1945)
Writers rejected traditional styles
            ↓
Experimental literature developed
New poetry, drama, novel techniques
            ↓
Result:
Psychological, realistic, and experimental literature
```

---

# 1️⃣ General Characteristics of Modern Age

### 🔹 Key Features & Keywords

* **Disillusionment and pessimism** 😔
  Result of World War I destruction

* **Psychological depth** 🧠
  Influence of Freud’s psychology

* **Experimental writing style** 🔬
  New literary techniques used

* **Break from tradition** ❌
  Rejected Victorian optimism

* **Focus on individual inner mind** 👤
  Thoughts and consciousness

* **Realism and harsh reality** ⚙️

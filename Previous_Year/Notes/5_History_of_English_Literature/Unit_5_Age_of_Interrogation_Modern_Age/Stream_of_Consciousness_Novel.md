# 🧠 Stream of Consciousness Novel

---

## 📖 1. Meaning of Stream of Consciousness

**Definition:**
**Stream of Consciousness is a narrative technique that presents a character’s continuous flow of thoughts, feelings, memories, and inner mental processes.**

👉 It shows the **mind directly**, without logical order.

**Easy Meaning:**
Stream of Consciousness = **writing that shows what a person is thinking inside their mind 🧠💭**

---

## 🧠 Origin of the Term

The term was first used by psychologist
William James

📅 **Exact date:** 1890
📘 Book: *The Principles of Psychology*

He said:

> Human thoughts flow continuously like a stream 🌊

---

## 📚 2. When Stream of Consciousness Novels Developed

📆 Period: **Modern Age (1900–1940)**

It developed because modern writers wanted to show:

* Inner thoughts 🧠
* Psychological reality 🤯
* Human consciousness 💭

---

## 1. James Joyce ⭐

### Major Work: 📘 *Ulysses*

📅 Published: **2 February 1922**

---

### Example (Exact Passage):

> “He was alone. He was unheeded, happy, and near to the wild heart of life.”

### Meaning:

* Shows inner feelings of character ❤️
* Shows psychological state 🧠

### Another famous stream passage:

> “Yes because he never did a thing like that before…”

This shows:

* Continuous thought flow
* No logical structure

---

### Keywords:

Stream of consciousness • Inner thoughts • Psychology

## 2. Virginia Woolf ⭐

### Major Work: 📘 *Mrs Dalloway*

📅 Published: **14 May 1925**

---

### Example passage:

* Memories 💭
* Emotions ❤️

Character moves between present and past

Meaning:

Shows excitement and inner emotion

Memory • Inner emotion • Psychological time

## 🧾 4. Main Characteristics / Features (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

### 🧠 1. Focus on Inner Thoughts

Shows character’s mind directly

Example:

Character thinks randomly

---

### ⏳ 2. No Logical Order

Thoughts jump between:

* Past 💭
* Present ⏱️
* Future 🔮

Example:

Mrs Dalloway remembers childhood suddenly

---

### 🧩 3. Fragmented Structure

Sentences incomplete or broken

Example:

Joyce’s writing uses broken thoughts

---

### 💭 4. Psychological Reality

Focus on mind, not external action

---

### 🔄 5. Free Flow of Thoughts

Like real thinking

Random and continuous

---

## 🧾 6. Structure and “Stanza-Like” Flow

Since novels don’t have stanzas, they use:

* Paragraph flow
* Thought flow
* Memory sequences

Example pattern:

Present moment ⏱️
↓
Memory 💭
↓
Emotion ❤️
↓
Return to present ⏱️

---

## 🧾 7. Purpose of Stream of Consciousness

Writers wanted to show:

* Real human thinking 🧠
* Psychological truth 🤯
* Inner reality 💭

Not just external events

## ⚖️ 8. Normal Novel vs Stream of Consciousness Novel

| Normal Novel 📖 | Stream of Consciousness Novel 🧠 |
| --------------- | -------------------------------- |
| External events | Internal thoughts                |
| Logical order   | Random order                     |
| Clear narration | Mental narration                 |
| Action focus    | Mind focus                       |

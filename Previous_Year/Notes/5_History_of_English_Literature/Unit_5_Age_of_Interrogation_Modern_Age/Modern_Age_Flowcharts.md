# 🌫️ The Modern Age (1890-1950) — Detailed Flowcharts, Authors, Movements & Keywords

This age reflects **war, doubt, psychological depth, and experimentation**. Old Victorian certainty collapsed, and writers explored the **human mind, alienation, and modern reality**.

---

# 🧭 OVERALL FLOWCHART OF MODERN AGE

```
Victorian confidence (order, morality)
            ↓
Industrialization + Science + Freud’s psychology 🧠
            ↓
World War I (1914–1918) 💣 → Loss, trauma, disillusionment
            ↓
Breakdown of traditional values ⚠️
            ↓
Experimentation in poetry, drama, and novels 🎭📖
            ↓
Modernism born → Psychological realism, symbolism, fragmented style
```

---

# ✨ GENERAL CHARACTERISTICS FLOWCHART

```
Victorian optimism ❌ rejected
        ↓
Disillusionment 😔
        ↓

        ↓
Experimental style (no fixed structure) 🔄
        ↓
Themes:

• Meaninglessness
```

**Keywords:** Modernism, realism, symbolism, pessimism, psychology, fragmentation

# 🟣 POETRY OF MODERN AGE

---

# 1️⃣ Transitional Poets Flowchart

These poets connect **Victorian → Modern poetry**

```
Victorian poetry (order, faith)
          ↓
Transitional poets introduce doubt & experiment
          ↓
Modern poetry fully experimental
```

### Main poets:

• Gerard Manley Hopkins
Keywords: sprung rhythm, religious struggle
Poem: *The Windhover*

• Robert Bridges
Keywords: classical style, transition poet

• W. B. Yeats
Keywords: symbolism, mysticism, Irish nationalism
Poem: *The Second Coming*

Example meaning:
"The Second Coming" → world chaos after WWI 💣

---

```
Industrial modern life ❌ rejected

          ↓

```

Main poets:

# 3️⃣ War Poets Flowchart 💣

```
World War I reality
      ↓
Soldiers experience horror
      ↓

• Death

# 4️⃣ Imagist Movement Flowchart 🖼️

```
Victorian long poetry ❌ rejected

Keywords:

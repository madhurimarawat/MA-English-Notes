# 📚 Identification of Stanzas

---

## (a)

> Ful wel she sung the service divine;
> Entuned in hir nose ful seemly,
> And Frensh she spake,
> Full faire and fetisly.

✅ **Poem:** The Canterbury Tales
✅ **Character:** The Prioress
✅ **Clue:** French speaking nun + nasal singing + Middle English

---

## (b)

> That fro tyme that he first bigan,
> To ridden out, he loved chivalrie,
> Trouthe and honour, Freedom and Curteisie.

✅ **Poem:** The Canterbury Tales
✅ **Character:** The Knight
✅ **Clue:** Keywords → chivalry, honour, courtesy ⚔️

---

## (c)

> The broad circumference
> Hung on his shoulders; like the moon, whose orb
> Through optic glass the Tuscan artist views

✅ **Poem:** Paradise Lost
✅ **Character:** Satan
✅ **Clue:** Epic simile comparing Satan’s shield to the moon 🌕

---

## (d)

> What though the field be lost?
> All is not lost; the unconquerable will,
> And courage never to submit or yield

✅ **Poem:** Paradise Lost
✅ **Speaker:** Satan
✅ **Clue:** Famous line about rebellion and strong will 😈

---

## (e)

> Think not, when women's transient breath is fled,
> That all her vanities at once are dead.

✅ **Poem:** The Rape of the Lock
✅ **Clue:** Satire on female vanity 💄

---

## (f)

> Oh thoughtless mortals! ever blind to fate,
> Too soon dejected, and too soon elate!

✅ **Poem:** The Rape of the Lock
✅ **Clue:** Mock-epic tone + fate + satire

---

## (g)

> My heart aches, and a drowsy numbness pains
> My sense, as though of hemlock I had drunk

✅ **Poem:** Ode to a Nightingale
✅ **Clue:** Numbness, sleep, drug imagery 🌙

---

## (h)

> A heart—how shall I say?—too soon made glad,
> Too easily impressed; she liked whate'er
> She looked on

✅ **Poem:** My Last Duchess
✅ **Speaker:** The Duke
✅ **Clue:** Jealous husband describing Duchess 😠

---

## (i)

> Fear death?—to feel the fog in my throat,
> The mist in my face

✅ **Poem:** Prospice
✅ **Clue:** Facing death bravely 💪

---

## (j)

> My mistress bent that brow of hers

✅ **Poem:** The Last Ride Together
✅ **Clue:** Rejected lover + mistress reference ❤️

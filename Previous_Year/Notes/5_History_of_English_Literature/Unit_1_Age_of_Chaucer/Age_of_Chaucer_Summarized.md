# 📜 The Age of Chaucer (1350–1400)

The Age of Chaucer marks the **beginning of English literature in its true form**. It is a **transition period** from medieval to modern thinking, language, and literary style.

---

# 1️⃣ Characteristic Features of the Age

### 🔹 Key Points (Exam-oriented)

* **Transition period** 🔄
  Between *Medieval Age* and *Renaissance*
  → Old beliefs ending, new ideas beginning

* **Rise of English language** 🇬🇧
  English replaced **Latin and French** in literature
  → Made literature accessible to common people

* **Growth of national identity** 🏰
  People started feeling proud of **English culture and language**

* **Social realism** 👥
  Literature showed **real life, real people, real society**
  → Not just religious stories

* **Influence of Church declining** ⛪⬇️
  Writers began criticizing corruption in Church

* **Focus on human life** ❤️
  Interest shifted from **only religion → human emotions, personality**

# 2️⃣ Development of Poetry in the Age of Chaucer

### 🔹 Key Points

* Poetry became the **dominant literary form** 📝

* **English poetry became refined and artistic** 🎨

* **New poetic styles introduced**

  * Rhyme
  * Rhythm
  * Narrative poetry

* **Courtly love theme popular** 💕
  Love, romance, and chivalry

* **Realistic poetry developed** 👥
  Showed merchants, knights, priests, workers

* Major contribution by Geoffrey Chaucer 👑
  → Considered greatest poet of the age

  * Priest ⛪

* Used **simple, natural, and realistic language**

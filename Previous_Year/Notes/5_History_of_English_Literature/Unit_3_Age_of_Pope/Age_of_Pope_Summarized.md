# 📜 The Age of Pope (1700–1750) — The Augustan Age / Age of Reason

This age is called the **Age of Pope** after its greatest poet, Alexander Pope 👑.
It is also known as the **Age of Reason** and **Age of Prose**, because literature focused on **logic, order, intellect, satire, and realism** rather than imagination.

### Augustan Age

* Called the **Augustan Age** because writers imitated the classical ideals of **order, balance, and reason** from the time of Augustus 🏛️

* The Roman Augustan period was a **golden age of literature**, and similarly, England produced great writers like Alexander Pope

* Writers focused on **satire, logic, discipline, and polished literary style** instead of imagination

* They followed **classical rules and structure**, inspired by Roman writers like Virgil and Horace

* Therefore, this period is called the Augustan Age because it was a **golden age of reason, refinement, and classical influence** 📖

During the **Age of Reason (Neoclassical Age)** — roughly 1660–1785 — writers consciously tried to imitate the classical ideals of ancient Greece and Rome: order, balance, reason, satire, decorum, and harmony.

## 🏛️ GREEK WRITERS

### 1. **Homer**

**Works imitated:**

* **Iliad**
* **Odyssey**

**Why imitated?**

* Epic structure
* Grand style
* Heroic themes

👉 Example: **Alexander Pope** translated Homer and imitated epic style in *The Rape of the Lock* (mock-epic form).

---

### 2. **Aristotle**

**Work imitated:**

* **Poetics**

**Why important?**

* Rules of tragedy
* Unity of time, place, and action
* Concept of catharsis

👉 Neoclassical dramatists followed these dramatic rules strictly.

---

### 3. **Sophocles**

**Work imitated:**

* **Oedipus Rex**

**Why imitated?**

* Tragic structure
* Moral seriousness
* Classical restraint

---

## 🏛️ ROMAN WRITERS

### 4. **Virgil**

**Work imitated:**

* **Aeneid**

**Why imitated?**

* National epic style
* Formal, polished language
* Patriotic themes

👉 English poets tried to create national epics like Virgil.

---

### 5. **Horace**

**Works imitated:**

* *Odes*
* *Ars Poetica*

**Why important?**

* Rules of poetry
* “Poetry should delight and instruct”
* Satirical tone

👉 Major influence on Augustan writers like Pope and Dryden.

---

### 6. **Juvenal**

**Work imitated:**

* *Satires*

**Why imitated?**

* Sharp social criticism
* Moral satire

👉 Influenced satirical writing in the Age of Reason.

---

## ✨ In Short (Exam-Ready Answer)

During the Age of Reason, writers imitated:

* **Homer** – *Iliad*, *Odyssey*
* **Aristotle** – *Poetics*
* **Sophocles** – *Oedipus Rex*
* **Virgil** – *Aeneid*
* **Horace** – *Odes*, *Ars Poetica*
* **Juvenal** – *Satires*

They imitated classical ideals of order, reason, satire, and decorum.

---

## ✨ Causes of Their Popularity

### 1️⃣ Rise of the Middle Class

* The growing middle class wanted literature.
* They were educated but not highly scholarly.
* Periodical essays were short, simple, and moral — perfect for them.

---

### 2️⃣ Growth of Coffee-House Culture ☕

* Coffee houses became centers of discussion.
* Newspapers and periodicals were read aloud and discussed.
* Essays became part of daily social life.

---

### 3️⃣ Increase in Literacy

* Education was spreading.
* More people could read.
* Demand for light, instructive reading increased.

---

### 4️⃣ Printing Press & Journalism Growth

* Printing became cheaper and faster.
* Regular publications became possible.
* Journalism developed as a profession.

---

### 5️⃣ Moral & Social Reform

* Writers like Addison and Steele wanted to:

  * Reform manners
  * Correct social vices
  * Promote politeness and morality
* They used humour and satire instead of harsh criticism.

---

### 6️⃣ Urban Life & Social Changes

* London society was expanding.
* People were interested in:

  * Fashion
  * Manners
  * Marriage
  * Social behavior
* Periodical essays discussed everyday life.

---

### 7️⃣ Easy & Attractive Style

* Clear prose.
* Conversational tone.
* No heavy classical language.
* Characters like “Sir Roger de Coverley” made essays entertaining.

---

## 📌 Short Exam Conclusion (3–4 lines)

The popularity of periodical essays in the Age of Pope was due to the rise of the middle class, spread of literacy, coffee-house culture, growth of printing, and the need for moral and social reform. Writers like Addison and Steele made literature simple, instructive, and entertaining, which appealed to common readers.

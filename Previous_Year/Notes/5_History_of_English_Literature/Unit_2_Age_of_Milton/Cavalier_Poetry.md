# 🎩 Cavalier Poetry

---

## 📖 1. Meaning of Cavalier Poetry

**Definition:**
**Cavalier Poetry** is the poetry written by the **Cavalier poets**, who supported King Charles I during the English Civil War(1642–1651).

> **Cavalier Poetry peak → 1625–1649**

👉 They wrote about **love ❤️, beauty 🌹, enjoyment of life 🍷, loyalty 👑, and living in the present (Carpe Diem)**.

**Easy Meaning:**
Cavalier poetry = **enjoy life now, love now, don’t worry about future or death**

---

## 🧠 2. Main Idea / Central Theme

### 🌟 Core Philosophy: **Carpe Diem**

**Carpe Diem = "Seize the Day" = Enjoy present moment**

**Main ideas include:**

* ❤️ Love and romance
* 🌹 Beauty of women
* 🍷 Pleasure and enjoyment
* ⏳ Shortness of life
* 👑 Loyalty to king
* 😌 Carefree attitude

**Keyword:**
`Carpe Diem • Love • Beauty • Pleasure • Loyalty • Present enjoyment`

---

## 🧾 3. Historical Background

📅 Time: **17th century (1600s)**
⚔️ Period: **English Civil War**

Two groups:

* 👑 Cavaliers → Supported King Charles I
* 🛐 Puritans → Supported Parliament

Cavalier poets belonged to the **royal court**, so their poetry is called **courtly poetry**

## ✍️ 4. Major Cavalier Poets (VERY IMPORTANT)

### 1. Robert Herrick ⭐ (MOST IMPORTANT)

**Famous poem:**
👉 *To the Virgins, to Make Much of Time*

**Famous quote:**

> “Gather ye rosebuds while ye may”

**Meaning:**
Enjoy youth before old age comes

**Keywords:**
Carpe Diem • Youth • Time passing • Beauty

---

### 2. Richard Lovelace

**Famous poem:**
👉 *To Althea, from Prison*

**Famous quote:**

> “Stone walls do not a prison make”

**Meaning:**
True freedom is freedom of mind

**Keywords:**
Freedom • Loyalty • Honor

**Keywords:**
Courtly love • Elegance • Refinement

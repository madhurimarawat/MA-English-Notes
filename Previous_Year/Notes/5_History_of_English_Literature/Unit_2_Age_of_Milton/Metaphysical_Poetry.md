# 🧠 Metaphysical Poetry

---

## 📖 1. Meaning of Metaphysical Poetry

**Definition:**
**Metaphysical Poetry** is a type of poetry that deals with **deep thinking about love ❤️, death 💀, soul 👻, God ✝️, and philosophy 🧠 using complex ideas and unusual comparisons (conceits).**

**Easy Meaning:**
Metaphysical poetry = **Poetry of deep thinking + brain + philosophy**

Metaphysical poetry is called “metaphysical” because it deals with **abstract (beyond physical) ideas** like love, soul, death, God, time, and existence — not just physical beauty or simple emotions.

### 🌿 Meaning of the word “Metaphysical”

* **Meta** = *beyond*
* **Physical** = *things of the physical world (body, matter, nature)*

So, **metaphysical = beyond the physical world**.

It comes from philosophy — especially ideas about reality, soul, God, and existence.

---

### 📚 Why is it called Metaphysical Poetry?

The term was later used by critic **Samuel Johnson** to describe poets like:

* **John Donne**
* **George Herbert**
* **Andrew Marvell**

These poets:

✔ Talked about **soul, God, death, time, eternity**
✔ Mixed **love with philosophy**
✔ Used strange but intelligent comparisons (called *conceits*)
✔ Used logical argument in poetry

---

### 🧠 Example

In *The Canonization* by **John Donne**, love is not just physical attraction — it becomes something spiritual and eternal.

In *The Collar* by **George Herbert**, the poet argues with God — very philosophical, very internal.

---

### ✨ Simple Definition:

**Metaphysical poetry is called so because it deals with ideas beyond the physical world — such as soul, God, love, death, and existence — using intellect and philosophical thinking.**  The term *Metaphysical Poetry* was first used by **Samuel Johnson** to describe a group of 17th-century poets like **John Donne**, **George Herbert**, and **Andrew Marvell**.

The word *metaphysical* means “beyond the physical.” These poets wrote about abstract and philosophical themes such as love, God, soul, death, time, and eternity rather than only physical beauty or simple emotions.

Their poetry is marked by:

* Intellectual and logical argument
* Use of wit and irony
* Unusual comparisons called **conceits**
* Blending of emotion with philosophy

Thus, it is called *Metaphysical Poetry* because it deals with ideas beyond the material world and reflects deep philosophical thinking.

---

## 📜 3. Origin and Background

📅 Time: Approx. 1590 – 1680 **17th century**

Metaphysical poetry flourished approximately between 1590 and 1680, with its golden period between 1600 and 1660 under the leadership of **John Donne**.

The term was first used by critic Samuel Johnson (18th century)

He said these poets used **too much intellect and strange comparisons**

## 🧾 5. Main Characteristics / Features

---

### 🧠 1. Intellectual Poetry

Focus on thinking and reasoning

Not emotional, but logical

---

### 🔗 2. Use of Conceits (VERY IMPORTANT KEYWORD)

**Conceit = unusual comparison**

Example:
John Donne compares lovers to a **compass**

Meaning:
Even if separated, they remain connected

---

### ❓ 3. Use of Questions

They ask deep philosophical questions

Example:
“What is death?”

---

### ✝️ 4. Religious Themes

God, sin, salvation, soul

---

### 💀 5. Death and Soul

Immortality of soul

Death is not final

---

### 📚 6. Complex Language

Harder than Cavalier poetry

## 🧾 7. Metaphysical Conceit Explained (VERY IMPORTANT)

Example: Compass conceit in Donne’s poem

Comparison:

* One lover = fixed leg
* Other lover = moving leg

Meaning:
True love remains connected even when apart

**Keyword:** Conceit

# 2️⃣ Puritan Movement

Puritans wanted **religious purity and moral discipline**

---

### 🔹 Key Features

* Opposed luxury and entertainment 🚫
* Supported simplicity and morality ✝️
* Influenced literature deeply

---

### 🔹 Major Writer

#### John Milton

**Major Work:**

* Paradise Lost

**Contribution:**

* Shows Puritan beliefs
* Theme of fall of man

### Why Milton supported Satan’s character but was still a Puritan

* 😈 **Satan appears heroic but is not supported by Milton** — In Paradise Lost, John Milton gives Satan strong speeches, courage, and leadership qualities, which make him seem like a tragic hero. This is a **literary technique to make the story powerful**, not to praise Satan.

* ✝️ **Milton’s main purpose was to justify God, not Satan** — Milton clearly states his aim is *“to justify the ways of God to men.”* The poem ultimately proves that **God is just, righteous, and supreme**, which reflects Milton’s strong **Puritan religious belief**.

* ⚖️ **Satan represents pride, rebellion, and disobedience** — These are shown as **dangerous sins**. Satan’s fall demonstrates the Puritan idea that **disobedience to God leads to punishment, suffering, and spiritual fall**.

* 📉 **Satan gradually becomes weaker and miserable** — At first he appears powerful, but later he becomes **confused, guilty, and defeated**, showing the **failure of evil and the victory of divine justice**, which supports Puritan morality.

* 🧠 **Milton uses Satan to teach a moral lesson** — Satan is used as a warning example. Milton shows that **ambition without obedience leads to destruction**, reinforcing Puritan values like **faith, humility, and obedience to God**.

# 5️⃣ Prose in Milton’s Age (1625–1660)

Prose became strong in religion and politics.

---

### 🔹 Major Writers

* John Milton — Areopagitica
* John Bunyan — Pilgrim’s Progress

Prose in the Age of Milton developed greatly due to **religious conflicts, political struggles, and the Puritan movement**. It became an important medium for expressing **religious beliefs, political opinions, and philosophical ideas**.

Unlike earlier decorative prose, Miltonic prose was:

* Serious and intellectual
* Argumentative and persuasive
* Religious and political in nature
* Clear, powerful, and logical

Writers used prose mainly to defend their ideas and influence society.

---

### 🔹 Major Writers

**1️⃣ John Milton**
**Major Work:** Areopagitica

**Contribution:**

* Defended freedom of speech and freedom of the press
* Wrote political and religious pamphlets supporting Puritan ideals
* His prose is scholarly, powerful, and persuasive
* He used prose as a tool to support liberty, truth, and moral values

Milton’s prose reflects his deep learning and strong political beliefs.

---

**2️⃣ John Bunyan**
**Major Work:** Pilgrim’s Progress

**Contribution:**

* Wrote religious prose in simple and clear language
* His work is a religious allegory showing the spiritual journey of a man
* Focused on faith, salvation, and moral struggle
* Made prose accessible to common people

Bunyan’s prose is simple, emotional, and spiritually powerful.

---

### 📊 Summary Table

| Writer | Work               | Contribution                                               |
| ------ | ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |

### ✅ Conclusion (2-line exam ending)

Thus, prose in the Age of Milton became a powerful medium for religious and political expression. Writers like Milton and Bunyan strengthened English prose through intellectual depth and spiritual themes.

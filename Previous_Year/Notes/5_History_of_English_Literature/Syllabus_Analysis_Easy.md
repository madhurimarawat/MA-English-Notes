# 📚 English Literature Syllabus Overview

---

## **Unit-I: The Age of Chaucer & The Age of Shakespeare**

### **1. The Age of Chaucer (1350–1400)**

**Topics to study:**

* Characteristic features of the age
* Development of poetry in the age of Chaucer
* Chaucer as the Father of English Poetry
* Development of prose during the age of Chaucer

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Chaucer’s life and contributions → essential and straightforward
* Poetry development → note examples like *The Canterbury Tales*
* Prose development → simpler as mostly historical context

---

### **2. The Age of Shakespeare (1558–1625)**

**Topics to study:**

* The Renaissance and its influence on Elizabethan literature
* Development of drama, poetry, and prose
* Shakespeare as the central figure of the period

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* The Renaissance influence → clear historical context
* Shakespeare’s works → easier if you memorize plays and genres (tragedy, comedy, history)

**Tips:** Remember Shakespeare → tragedies (*Hamlet*, *Macbeth*), comedies (*A Midsummer Night’s Dream*), histories (*Henry IV*).

---

## **Unit-II: The Age of Milton & The Restoration**

### **1. The Age of Milton (1625–1660)**

**Topics to study:**

* Salient literary trends
* University Wits & Pre-Shakespearean drama
* Elizabethan sonnets and sonneteers
* Development of prose
* Puritan movement
* Metaphysical poetry (Donne, Herbert)
* Cavalier poetry
* Prose in Milton’s age

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Metaphysical & Cavalier poetry → key poets and style differences
* Milton → *Paradise Lost*, style and themes
* Prose → easier to note examples

---

### **2. The Restoration Period (1660–1700)**

**Topics to study:**

* Social, political, literary tendencies
* Restoration satire & satirists (Dryden, Pope)
* Comedy of manners & dramatists
* English novel in the latter 17th century

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Satire & comedy → easy to remember via key authors
* Social context → helpful for multiple-choice

---

## **Unit-III: The Age of Pope & Dr. Johnson**

### **1. The Age of Pope (1700–1750)**

**Topics to study:**

* Literary characteristics of the age
* 18th century as an age of prose and reason
* Growth of periodical essays
* *Coverley Papers* as first sketch of the English novel

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Periodical essays → Steele & Addison
* Pope → *Essay on Man*, style, satire

---

### **2. The Age of Transition / Dr. Johnson (1750–1798)**

**Topics to study:**

* Salient features of Romantic revival poetry
* Precursors of Romantic revival (poets of revolt)
* French Revolution & influence on English literature
* "Four wheels" of the novel of the 18th century

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Dr. Johnson → dictionary & essays → easier
* Poets of revolt → note key names like Blake, Cowper

---

## **Unit-IV: Romanticism & Victorian Age**

### **1. The Age of Romanticism (1798–1832)**

**Topics to study:**

* Characteristics of Romanticism
* Prose of Romantic age
* Novels of Romantic age

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Romantic poets → Wordsworth, Coleridge, Shelley → poems & themes

---

### **2. The Victorian Age (1832–1887)**

**Topics to study:**

* Salient features of Victorian poetry
* Spasmodic school of poetry
* Pre-Raphaelite movement & chief exponents
* Oxford Movement
* Victorian novels & novelists

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Victorian novels → Dickens, Thackeray → easier to remember
* Poetry movements → focus on examples & characteristics

## **Unit-V: Modern Age / Age of Interrogation (1890–1950)**

### **Topics to study:**

* General characteristics of the age

* Poetry:

  * Transitional poets → Bridges, Hopkins, Yeats
  * Georgian poets
  * War poets
  * Imagist movement & exponents
  * Neo-metaphysical poetry

* Essays & essayists

* Drama:

  * Expressionistic school
  * Problem play
  * Poetic drama
  * Theatre of the absurd

* Stream of consciousness novel

**Focus / Easier topics:**

* Poetry movements → key poets and their style
* Drama → note types & examples (e.g., Ibsen, Beckett)
* Stream of consciousness → Joyce, Woolf

### **1️⃣ When a century starts**

* Centuries are counted **starting from year 1**, not year 0.
* So:

| Century      | Years (Exact) |
| ------------ | ------------- |
| 14th century | 1301 – 1400   |
| 15th century | 1401 – 1500   |
| 16th century | 1501 – 1600   |

💡 **Tip:** The “hundreds” we see (1300, 1400, etc.) are just approximate markers; the exact century **always starts at `01` and ends at `00`**.

* **1300** → Last year of the **13th century**
* **1301** → First year of the **14th century**

So the **14th century runs from 1301 to 1400**.

---

### How to decide Early / Late Century (Halves)

* **1️⃣ Divide the century into two equal parts:**

  * **Early:** Years 01–50
  * **Late:** Years 51–00 (or 51–100)
    **Example:** 1401–1500 (15th century)
  * 1401–1450 → Early 15th century
  * 1451–1500 → Late 15th century

---

#### How to decide Early / Late Century 🕰️

* **1️⃣ Early Century 🌱**

  * Think **“just started”** → first half of the century
  * Roughly years **01 to 50**
  * **Example:** 1401–1450 → Early 15th century
  * Emoji tip: 🌱 = beginning of growth

* **2️⃣ Late Century 🍂**

  * Think **“almost ending / finishing”** → second half of the century
  * Roughly years **51 to 00**
  * **Example:** 1451–1500 → Late 15th century
  * Emoji tip: 🍂 = leaves falling / nearing the end

💡 **Shortcut trick:**

* First half of the century → Early 🌱
* Second half → Late 🍂

---

### How to decide Early / Mid / Late Century

* **1️⃣ Divide the century into three equal parts:**

  * **Early:** Years 01–33
  * **Mid:** Years 34–66
  * **Late:** Years 67–00 (or 67–100)
    **Example:** 1200–1300 (13th century)
  * 1201–1233 → Early 13th century
  * 1234–1266 → Mid 13th century
  * 1267–1300 → Late 13th century

#### How to decide Early / Mid / Late Century 🕰️

* **1️⃣ Early Century 🌱**

  * Think **“just started”** → first part of the century
  * Roughly years **01 to 33**
  * **Example:** 1201–1233 → Early 13th century
  * Emoji tip: 🌱 = beginning of growth

* **2️⃣ Mid Century 🌿**

  * Think **“middle” / “halfway through”** → middle years of the century
  * Roughly years **34 to 66**
  * **Example:** 1234–1266 → Mid 13th century
  * Emoji tip: 🌿 = full growth / mature stage

* **3️⃣ Late Century 🍂**

  * Think **“almost ending / finishing”** → last years of the century
  * Roughly years **67 to 00**
  * **Example:** 1267–1300 → Late 13th century
  * Emoji tip: 🍂 = leaves falling / nearing the end

💡 **Shortcut trick:**

* First third of the century → Early 🌱
* Second third → Mid 🌿
* Last third → Late 🍂

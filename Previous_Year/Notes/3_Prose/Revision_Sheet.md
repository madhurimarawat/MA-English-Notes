# **Prose Revision Sheet**

## **Of Truth / Of Studies / Of Friendship / Of Great Place** – Francis Bacon

* **Age/Era:** Elizabethan / Early 17th Century
* **Type:** Aphoristic Essay, Didactic
* **Main Idea:**

  * **Of Truth** – Importance of truth in life.
  * **Of Studies** – Value and use of reading and learning.
  * **Of Friendship** – Benefits of friendship.
  * **Of Great Place** – Duties and responsibilities of people in high positions.
* **Key Focus:** Practical wisdom and concise moral teaching.

---

## **Sir Roger de Coverley Papers (Nos. 110, 112, 117, 119)** – Joseph Addison & Richard Steele

* **Age/Era:** Neoclassical Age (18th Century)
* **Type:** Periodical Essay, Social Satire
* **Main Idea:** Description of Sir Roger and 18th-century English society with gentle humor.
* **Main Characters:** Sir Roger de Coverley, Spectator (narrator), Will Honeycomb.

---

## **Dream Children: A Reverie** – Charles Lamb

* **Age/Era:** Romantic Age (19th Century)
* **Type:** Personal Essay, Sentimental
* **Main Idea:** A dream about imaginary children reflecting Lamb’s lost love and loneliness.
* **Main Focus:** Alice (dream child), Lamb’s childhood memories.

---

## **Christ’s Hospital Five and Thirty Years Ago** – Charles Lamb

* **Age/Era:** Romantic Age
* **Type:** Autobiographical Essay
* **Main Idea:** School life memories; contrast between Lamb and Coleridge’s experiences.
* **Main Focus:** School discipline and childhood struggles.

---

## **Hero as Poet** – Thomas Carlyle

* **Age/Era:** Victorian Age (19th Century)
* **Type:** Lecture Essay, Philosophical
* **Main Idea:** Poets are prophets and spiritual leaders of society.
* **Main Focus:** Dante and Shakespeare as ideal poet-heroes.

---

## **Utopia** – Thomas More

* **Age/Era:** Renaissance / Early 16th Century
* **Type:** Political / Philosophical Prose, Satire
* **Main Idea:** Description of an ideal imaginary society with equality and justice.
* **Main Characters:** Raphael Hythloday, Thomas More (narrator).

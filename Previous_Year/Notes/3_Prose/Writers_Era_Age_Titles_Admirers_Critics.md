# Writers Era, Age, Titles, Admirers, Critics, Contribution

# 🟣 Francis Bacon

### 📅 Era & Age

* Elizabethan Age
* Late 16th – Early 17th Century
* Early prose writer of English Renaissance

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1561**, London, England
* Lawyer, philosopher, statesman
* Became Lord Chancellor of England
* Died: **1626**, due to illness (experiment with freezing meat)

### 📚 Famous Works

* *Essays* (Of Truth, Of Studies, Of Friendship, Of Revenge)
* *The Advancement of Learning*
* *Novum Organum*
* *New Atlantis*

### 🏆 Titles & Who Gave Them

* **“Father of English Essay”** – literary critics
* **“Father of Empiricism”** – philosophers

### 👏 Admirers

* Influenced Enlightenment thinkers
* Admired for practical wisdom

### ⚖️ Critics

* Accused of corruption in political career
* Some critics call his essays too concise and aphoristic

### 🌟 Contribution

* Introduced aphoristic essay style
* Developed scientific method (inductive reasoning)
* Made English prose clear, direct, and logical

# 🟣 Joseph Addison

### 📅 Era & Age

* Augustan Age
* Early 18th Century

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1672**, England
* Educated at Oxford
* Statesman and essayist
* Died: **1719**

### 📚 Famous Works

* *The Spectator* (with Steele)
* *The Tatler*
* *Cato* (tragedy)

### 🏆 Titles

* **“Master of English Prose Style”** – critics

### 👏 Admirers

* Praised by Samuel Johnson
* Respected for moral essays

### ⚖️ Critics

* Sometimes called overly moralizing

### 🌟 Contribution

* Polished periodical essay
* Improved middle-class taste and manners
* Brought balance and clarity to prose

---

# 🟣 Richard Steele

### 📅 Era & Age

* Augustan Age
* Early 18th Century

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1672**, Dublin, Ireland
* Soldier, journalist, dramatist
* Died: **1729**

### 📚 Famous Works

* *The Tatler* (founder)
* *The Spectator* (with Addison)
* *The Conscious Lovers*

### 🏆 Titles

* **Pioneer of Periodical Journalism** – critics

### 👏 Admirers

* Worked closely with Addison
* Appreciated for emotional and humane style

### ⚖️ Critics

* Considered less polished than Addison

### 🌟 Contribution

* Founded modern periodical essay
* Focused on social reform and morality
* Made literature accessible to common readers

---

## 🟢 Combined Contribution of Addison & Steele

* Founded English periodical essay tradition
* Improved social manners of middle class
* Promoted moral and ethical values
* Made literature conversational and accessible
* Shaped 18th-century journalism

# 🟣 Charles Lamb

### 📅 Era & Age

* Romantic Age
* Late 18th – Early 19th Century

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1775**, London
* Faced family tragedy (sister Mary Lamb)
* Died: **1834**

### 📚 Famous Works

* *Essays of Elia*
* *Tales from Shakespeare* (with Mary Lamb)

### 🏆 Titles

* **“Prince of English Essayists”** – critics

### 👏 Admirers

* Admired by Wordsworth and Coleridge

### ⚖️ Critics

* Sometimes considered too personal and nostalgic

### 🌟 Contribution

* Perfected personal essay
* Added humour, pathos, and intimacy to prose
* Made essay more autobiographical

# 🟣 Thomas More


### 📅 Era & Age

* Renaissance Period
* 16th Century
* Associated with Humanism

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1478**, London, England
* A lawyer, scholar, social philosopher, and statesman
* Close friend of Desiderius Erasmus
* Served as Lord Chancellor under Henry VIII
* Executed in **1535** for refusing to accept the King as Supreme Head of the Church of England

### 📚 Famous Work

* *Utopia* (1516)

### 🏆 Titles

* Canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church (1935)
* Known as a great Renaissance humanist

### 👏 Admirers

* Desiderius Erasmus greatly admired him and praised his intellect and character.
* Respected by later historians as a man of strong moral principles.
* Honored as a saint by the Catholic Church.

### ⚖️ Critics

* Some Protestant historians criticized him for opposing the Reformation.
* Accused of supporting harsh measures against religious reformers.
* Some critics view *Utopia* as impractical or unrealistic.

### 🌟 Contribution

* Introduced the concept of an ideal society in *Utopia*
* Criticized social injustice, corruption, and inequality in England
* Major figure in Renaissance humanism
* Influenced political philosophy and social thought

### ⚖️ Importance

Thomas More is remembered for his moral integrity, humanist ideals, and courageous stand for conscience over political pressure.

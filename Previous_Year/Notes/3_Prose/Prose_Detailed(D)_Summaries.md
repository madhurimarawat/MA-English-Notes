# 📘 1. Francis Bacon – Selected Essays

(From *Essays* – Francis Bacon)

## 🔹 Summary

Bacon’s essays are short, aphoristic, and practical. They provide moral and intellectual guidance for life.

* **Of Studies** → Benefits and uses of reading.
* **Of Truth** → Nature of truth and human tendency to lie.
* **Of Friendship** → Explains the importance of friendship in reducing sorrow, giving advice, and supporting emotional balance.
* **Of Great Place** → Responsibilities of people in power.

He focuses on practical wisdom, worldly success, and moral discipline.

## 🔺 Climax

No narrative climax — intellectual climax comes in the concluding maxims of each essay.

## 🔹 Themes

* Practical wisdom
* Knowledge and learning
* Truth vs falsehood
* Power and responsibility
* Moral restraint

## 🔹 Key Points

* Reading shapes judgment.
* Truth is divine but humans love lies.
* Revenge makes a man equal to his enemy.
* Power requires service and sacrifice.
* Friendship relieves emotional suffering and provides counsel.

## ⭐ Important Quotes

* “Reading maketh a full man; conference a ready man; and writing an exact man.”
* “A lie faces God and shrinks from man.”
* “A principal fruit of friendship is the ease and discharge of the fullness of the heart.”

# 📘 2. Addison & Steele – Coverley Papers

(From *The Spectator* – Joseph Addison & Richard Steele)

## 🔹 Summary

The Coverley Papers present the character of Sir Roger de Coverley, a country gentleman. Through humorous and gentle satire, the essays portray 18th-century English society, domestic life, manners, and moral behavior.

Sir Roger is kind but slightly eccentric. His interactions with servants, church members, and townspeople show social values and human weakness.

## 🔺 Climax

There is no dramatic climax. Emotional high point is Sir Roger’s death — evoking sympathy and nostalgia.

## 🔹 Themes

* Social manners and civility
* Domestic life
* Humor with moral lesson
* Balance between tradition and change

## 🔹 Main Character

* **Sir Roger de Coverley** – Kind, traditional, humorous country squire

## ⭐ Important Quotes

* “A man may be happy in a humble station.”
* “True wit is nature to advantage dressed.”

# 📘 3. Charles Lamb – Selected Essays

*(From* Essays of Elia*)*

### Essays:

* *Dream Children: A Reverie*
* *A Bachelor's Complaint of the Behaviour of Married People*
* *Christ's Hospital Five and Thirty Years Ago*

---

## 🔹 Summary

Charles Lamb writes personal, nostalgic, reflective, and humorous essays under the pen name **Elia**. His essays combine emotion with gentle humor and often draw from his own life.

* **Dream Children** → A deeply emotional reflection on lost love and unfulfilled family life.
* **A Bachelor’s Complaint** → A humorous yet ironic complaint about how married friends neglect bachelors.
* **Christ’s Hospital** → A nostalgic recollection of Lamb’s school days, highlighting discipline, friendship, and hardship.

His style is conversational, emotional, intimate, and reflective.

---

## 🔺 Climax

In *Dream Children*, the climax comes when the children suddenly disappear, revealing that they were only imaginary figures of Lamb’s dream — symbolizing his loneliness and lost happiness.

---

## 🔹 Themes

* Memory and nostalgia
* Loneliness and loss
* Childhood and innocence
* Humor with pathos
* Personal reflection

---

## 🔹 Key Elements

* Blend of humor and sadness (pathos)
* Autobiographical tone
* Gentle irony
* Simple and conversational language

---

## ⭐ Important Quotes

* “We are nothing; less than nothing, and dreams.” (*Dream Children*)
* “I remember L. at school…” (*Christ’s Hospital*)
* “Children love to listen to stories about their elders.” (*Dream Children*)

# 📘 4. A.G. Gardiner – Selected Essays

(From *Leaves in the Wind* – A. G. Gardiner)

### Essays: On Saying Please, On the Rule of the Road

## 🔹 Summary

Gardiner emphasizes everyday civility and social responsibility.

* **On Saying Please** → Politeness costs nothing but creates harmony.
* **On the Rule of the Road** → Freedom must be balanced by discipline.

He uses simple language and practical illustrations.

## 🔺 Climax

In *On Saying Please*, the dismissal of the rude lift-man shows consequences of incivility.

## 🔹 Themes

* Civility and manners
* Social responsibility
* Discipline and freedom
* Moral behavior in daily life

## 🔹 Key Ideas

* Courtesy oils the machinery of society.
* Liberty without restraint leads to chaos.

## ⭐ Important Quotes

* “Politeness is the oil that lubricates the machinery of society.”
* “A reasonable consideration for others is the foundation of good manners.”

# 📘 5. Michel de Montaigne – Selected Essays

(From *Essais* – Michel de Montaigne)

### Essays: Of Idleness, Of Readie or Slow Speech, That We Should Not Judge Our Happiness Until After Our Death

## 🔹 Summary

Montaigne reflects on human nature and philosophy.

* **Of Idleness** → An idle mind produces uncontrolled thoughts.
* **Of Readie or Slow Speech** → Speaking ability varies; both have value.
* **Happiness After Death** → A man’s life can be judged only after death.

He writes in a personal, reflective, philosophical tone.

## 🔺 Climax

No narrative climax — philosophical conclusion emphasizes life’s uncertainty.

## 🔹 Themes

* Self-examination
* Moderation
* Human weakness
* True happiness

## 🔹 Key Ideas

* Idleness breeds mental chaos.
* Wisdom requires reflection.
* Life must be judged as a whole.

## ⭐ Important Quotes

* “The mind that has no fixed aim loses itself.”
* “Call no man happy till he is dead.”

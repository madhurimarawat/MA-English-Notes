# Coverley Papers Essays

# 🟣 ESSAY NO. 1

## “The Spectator’s Account of Himself”

### 🔹 Introduction

This essay introduces the narrator “Mr. Spectator” and the Spectator Club.

### 🔹 Beginning

The Spectator describes:

* His silent nature
* His habit of observing people
* His education and travels

### 🔹 Development

He explains why he does not speak much and prefers observation.
Then he introduces the club members, especially **Sir Roger de Coverley**.

### 🔹 Ending

He says he will use the paper to reform society and improve manners.

---

### 🔹 Themes

* Observation of society
* Social reform
* Moderation and balance
* Urban life

---

### 🔹 Character Analysis

**Mr. Spectator**

* Silent observer
* Intelligent
* Neutral and balanced
* Represents reason

**Sir Roger (brief introduction here)**

* Kind but eccentric
* Old-fashioned country gentleman

---

### 🔹 Significance

* Beginning of periodical essay tradition
* Introduces character sketch
* Foundation of The Coverley Papers

---

### 🔹 Important Line (idea)

“I have observed, that a Reader seldom peruses a Book with Pleasure, till he knows whether the Writer of it be a black or a fair Man…”

(Shows curiosity about personality and identity.)

# 🟣 ESSAY NO. 110

## “On Ghosts and Apparitions”

### 🔹 Introduction

Essay about belief in ghosts and superstitions.

### 🔹 Beginning

Addison discusses why people believe in ghosts.

### 🔹 Development (Climax)

He argues:

* Fear and imagination create ghosts.
* Weak minds believe easily.
* Superstition spreads through ignorance.

### 🔹 Ending

He promotes rational thinking instead of blind belief.

---

### 🔹 Themes

* Superstition vs Reason
* Fear psychology
* Enlightenment thinking

---

### 🔹 Character Aspect

Sir Roger believes in ghosts (traditional thinking).
The Spectator represents rational thought.

---

### 🔹 Significance

* Reflects Age of Enlightenment
* Encourages scientific thinking
* Criticizes blind belief

---

### 🔹 Important Idea

“Fear gives birth to imaginary beings.”

---

# 🟣 ESSAY NO. 112

## “Sir Roger at Church”

### 🔹 Introduction

Description of Sir Roger’s behavior at church.

### 🔹 Beginning

Sir Roger takes the narrator to his village church.

### 🔹 Development (Climax)

We see:

* He controls seating arrangement.
* He corrects villagers.
* He gives prayer books to poor people.
* He shows authority and kindness.

This is both comic and admirable.

### 🔹 Ending

The Spectator praises Sir Roger’s goodness.

---

### 🔹 Themes

* Religion
* Rural life
* Authority and kindness
* Social order

---

### 🔹 Character Analysis: Sir Roger

* Generous
* Protective
* Conservative
* Slightly comic

---

### 🔹 Significance

* Ideal picture of 18th-century country gentleman
* Shows moral leadership

---

### 🔹 Important Line (idea)

He “would often stand up when he was not called to it.”

(Shows his eagerness and comic personality.)

# 🟣 ESSAY NO. 117

## “On Witchcraft – Story of Moll White”

### 🔹 Introduction

Essay about superstition and witchcraft.

### 🔹 Beginning

Villagers accuse an old woman, Moll White, of being a witch.

### 🔹 Development (Climax)

Sir Roger believes she is a witch.
The Spectator doubts it.
Moll White becomes a victim of social fear.

### 🔹 Ending

Essay indirectly criticizes blind superstition.

---

### 🔹 Themes

* Superstition
* Social injustice
* Fear psychology
* Ignorance

---

### 🔹 Character Analysis

**Moll White**

* Poor old woman
* Social outcast
* Victim of superstition

**Sir Roger**

* Good but superstitious

---

### 🔹 Significance

* Social criticism
* Enlightenment values
* Sympathy for marginalized people

# 🔵 One-Line Conclusion (Exam Ready)

Sir Roger de Coverley is a kind-hearted, old-fashioned country gentleman who symbolizes traditional rural England and represents a harmonious blend of authority, morality, humour, and human weakness.

---

## 🟣 6 Points on Sir Roger’s Humour

### 1️⃣ Comic Authority in Church

He behaves like the “manager” of the church.

* Tells people when to stand.
* Corrects them loudly.
* Sometimes stands up when not required.

👉 This creates gentle comedy.

---

### 2️⃣ His Superstition

He believes in:

* Ghosts
* Witches (Moll White)

But he believes innocently, not wickedly.
👉 His fear makes him humorous.

---

### 3️⃣ His Love for the Widow

* He still loves her after rejection.
* Pretends not to care but clearly cares deeply.
  👉 His shy romantic behaviour is sweetly comic.

---

### 4️⃣ His Over-Protectiveness

* Protects his servants too much.
* Gets angry if someone disrespects them.

👉 His seriousness in small matters becomes humorous.

---

### 5️⃣ His Old-Fashioned Views

* Dislikes modern city manners.
* Prefers rural customs.
* Slightly confused by new ideas.

👉 His traditional mindset creates situational humour.

---

### 6️⃣ His Innocent Eccentricity

He is:

* Slightly dramatic
* Emotionally expressive
* Sometimes overly serious

But never evil or foolish.
👉 Readers laugh with him, not at him.

---

# 🟢 One-Line Exam Conclusion

Sir Roger’s humour arises from his innocence, superstition, romantic sensitivity, and exaggerated sense of authority, making him a lovable and human comic character.

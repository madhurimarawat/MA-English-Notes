# Prose Writers Writing Styles Summary

# ✨ 1. Francis Bacon – Writing Style

👉 **Age:**

> Bacon belongs to the **Elizabethan (1558–1603)** and **Jacobean (1603–1625)** periods.

### 🔹 Key Features:

* **Aphoristic Style**
  Short, crisp, quote-like sentences (easy to remember).

* **Concise & Compressed**
  Maximum meaning in minimum words.

* **Didactic (Moral Teaching)**
  Gives practical wisdom for life.

* **Use of Antithesis**
  Balanced opposites (e.g., “Reading maketh a full man…”).

* **Formal & Impersonal**
  No personal emotions — objective tone.

* **Use of Classical References**
  Draws from history, mythology.

### 🧠 In one line:

> Bacon’s style is **compact, intellectual, and full of practical wisdom**.

---

# ✨ 2. Joseph Addison – Writing Style

👉 **Age:**

> Addison belongs to the **Augustan / Neoclassical Age (c. 1700–1750)**, often called the Age of Pope.

### 🌿 Key Features:

* **Clear & Elegant**
  Extremely smooth, polished, and easy to understand.

* **Balanced & Refined**
  No exaggeration — everything is controlled and graceful.

* **Moral & Didactic**
  Teaches values (manners, morality) in a gentle way.

* **Conversational yet Polished**
  Feels like speaking to educated society.

* **Humor (Light & Polite)**
  Subtle humor — never harsh or offensive.

* **Use of Imagery & Description**
  Makes ideas vivid but not heavy.

👉 His essays in *The Spectator* aim to **reform society politely**.

### 🧠 In one line:

> Addison’s style is **graceful, clear, and morally instructive with refined humor**.

---

# ✨ 3. Richard Steele – Writing Style

👉 **Age:**

> Steele also belongs to the **Augustan / Neoclassical Age (c. 1700–1750)**.

### 🔥 Key Features:

* **Less Polished, More Emotional**
  Compared to Addison, Steele is more spontaneous.

* **Warm & Human**
  Focus on everyday life, feelings, and social issues.

* **Energetic & Lively**
  His writing has movement and enthusiasm.

* **Sentimental Tone**
  Shows sympathy, especially for common people.

* **Conversational & Direct**
  Feels more natural, less refined than Addison.

👉 He often brings **life and emotion**, while Addison adds **polish and perfection**.

### 🧠 In one line:

> Steele’s style is **lively, emotional, and less formal, full of human warmth**.

---

# ⚡ Addison vs Steele (Important Contrast)

| Feature | Addison             | Steele                 |
| ------- | ------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Style   | Polished & refined  | Natural & spontaneous  |
| Tone    | Calm, balanced      | Emotional, lively      |
| Humor   | Subtle & controlled | Open & expressive      |
| Focus   | Manners & morality  | Social life & feelings |

---

# 🔥 Final Combined Line (Exam Gold)

> Addison brings elegance and refinement to prose, while Steele adds warmth and liveliness; together, they create a perfect blend of moral instruction and engaging social commentary.

---

# ✨ 4. Charles Lamb – Writing Style

👉 **Age:**

> Lamb belongs to the **Romantic Age (1798–1832)**.

### 🌸 Key Features:

* **Personal & Familiar**
  Feels like a friendly conversation.

* **Humorous & Playful**
  Gentle humor, often self-deprecating.

* **Emotional & Nostalgic**
  Remembers childhood, past experiences with warmth.

* **Use of Archaic Words**
  Slight old-fashioned flavor.

* **Imaginative & Whimsical**
  Blends reality with imagination.

* **Subjective**
  Full of personal opinions and feelings.

### 🧠 In one line:

> Lamb’s style is **personal, humorous, and deeply emotional**.

---

# ✨ 5. Thomas Carlyle – Writing Style

👉 **Age:**

> Carlyle belongs to the **Victorian Age (1832–1887)**.

### 🔥 Key Features:

* **Oratorical & Prophetic**
  His prose feels like a speech — intense, commanding, almost sermon-like.

* **Emotional & Passionate**
  Not calm like Bacon — he writes with **fire, urgency, and conviction**.

* **Complex & Dense**
  Long sentences, unusual structures, sometimes difficult to follow.

* **Use of Exclamations & Questions**
  Makes writing dramatic and engaging.

* **Symbolism & Imagery**
  Uses strong metaphors (heroes, light, truth, etc.).

* **Moral Seriousness**
  Focuses on truth, heroism, and spiritual values.

### 🧠 In one line:

> Carlyle’s style is **passionate, prophetic, and forceful**, aiming to inspire and awaken.

---

# ✨ 6. Thomas More – Writing Style

👉 **Age:**

> More belongs to the **Renaissance / Early Tudor Period (c. 1485–1660)**.

### 🔹 Key Features:

* **Simple & Clear**
  Easy to understand compared to later writers.

* **Humanistic Tone**
  Focus on society, justice, and human values.

* **Satirical & Critical**
  Especially in *Utopia* — criticizes real society indirectly.

* **Balanced & Logical**
  Uses reason and structured arguments.

* **Idealistic**
  Describes a perfect society to highlight flaws in the real one.

### 🧠 In one line:

> More’s style is **clear, logical, and satirical, promoting humanist ideals**.

---

| Writer      | Style Type | Tone       | Key Trait            |
| ----------- | ---------- | ---------- | -------------------- |
| **Bacon**   | Aphoristic | Formal     | Wisdom in short form |
| **Lamb**    | Familiar   | Warm       | Personal & nostalgic |
| **Carlyle** | Oratorical | Passionate | Heroic & intense     |
| **More**    | Logical    | Balanced   | Satirical humanism   |

---

# 🔥 Final Combined Line (Use in Answers)

> While Bacon writes with concise wisdom, Carlyle thunders with prophetic intensity; More employs logical satire to critique society, whereas Lamb delights with personal, emotional, and humorous prose.

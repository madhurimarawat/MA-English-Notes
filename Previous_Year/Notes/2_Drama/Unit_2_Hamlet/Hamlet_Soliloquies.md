# Hamlet Soliloquies

## What Is a Soliloquy? 

The term “soliloquy” ( so-lil-o-quee) is generally used as a means of character revelation or character manifestation to the audience or the reader of the drama. Soliloquy is used as a tool or process by which the dramatist conveys the secret thoughts and/or intentions of the character, to the audience or the reader of the drama, but; also while doing so, it preserves the secrecy of those thoughts from the other characters of that drama.

A soliloquy is generally made when the character is alone, or when he/she thinks to be alone, in order to preserve the secrecy from other characters.

## 🎭 What is a Soliloquy? (Definition)

**Soliloquy = when a character speaks alone on stage and reveals inner thoughts and feelings.**

👉 Purpose:

* Shows psychology 🧠
* Shows emotions ❤️
* Shows conflict ⚔️

---

Hamlet has **7 major soliloquies** showing his mental development.

## 1️⃣ First Soliloquy

📍 Act 1, Scene 2
Opening line:

> “O that this too too solid flesh would melt”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning (Easy)

Hamlet is extremely depressed 😔

He wishes his body would melt and disappear.

He is suffering because:

* His father died 💀
* His mother married Claudius too quickly 💔

He feels life is meaningless.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords

Depression • Grief • Disgust • Death wish • Emotional suffering

---

#### 🔍 Important Symbol

**Flesh melting = desire to escape life**

---

#### 🧾 Exam Line

This soliloquy shows Hamlet’s depression and disgust at his mother’s remarriage.

---

## 2️⃣ Second Soliloquy

📍 Act 1, Scene 5
Opening line:

> “O all you host of heaven!”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning

Hamlet has just met his father’s ghost 👻

The ghost revealed:

Claudius murdered him.

Hamlet is:

* Shocked 😨
* Angry 😡
* Determined to take revenge ⚔️

He promises to remember only revenge.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords

Revenge • Duty • Shock • Anger • Supernatural influence

---

#### Symbol

**Ghost = truth and revenge**

---

#### Exam Line

This soliloquy marks Hamlet’s decision to seek revenge.

---

## 3️⃣ Third Soliloquy

📍 Act 2, Scene 2
Opening line:

> “O, what a rogue and peasant slave am I!”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning

Hamlet criticizes himself.

He feels cowardly because:

* He has not yet taken revenge

He compares himself to an actor 🎭

Actor shows fake emotion, but Hamlet cannot act on real emotion.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords

Self-criticism • Guilt • Delay • Frustration

---

#### Symbol

**Actor = contrast between action and inaction**

---

#### Exam Line

Hamlet blames himself for his inaction and delay in revenge.

---

## 4️⃣ Fourth Soliloquy (MOST IMPORTANT)

📍 Act 3, Scene 1
Opening line:

> “To be, or not to be”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning

Hamlet thinks about:

* Life vs Death
* Suicide vs survival

He wonders:

Is it better to suffer or die?

He fears death because it is unknown.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords (VERY IMPORTANT)

Existential crisis • Suicide • Life vs death • Fear of unknown • Philosophy

---

#### Symbol

**Sleep = Death**

Death compared to sleep.

---

#### Exam Line (VERY IMPORTANT)

This soliloquy shows Hamlet’s philosophical conflict about life and death.

---

## 5️⃣ Fifth Soliloquy

📍 Act 3, Scene 2

Opening line:

> “'Tis now the very witching time of night”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning

Hamlet is ready to confront his mother.

He feels anger 😡

But he decides:

He will use words, not violence.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords

Anger • Self-control • Revenge preparation

---

#### Symbol

**Night = evil and revenge**

---

#### Exam Line

Hamlet prepares mentally to confront his mother but chooses restraint.

---

## 6️⃣ Sixth Soliloquy

📍 Act 3, Scene 3

Opening line:

> “Now might I do it pat”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning

Hamlet sees Claudius praying 🙏

He has a chance to kill him.

But he decides NOT to kill him.

Why?

Because Claudius may go to Heaven.

Hamlet wants him punished in Hell.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords

Revenge delay • Moral conflict • Religious belief

---

#### Symbol

**Prayer = salvation**

---

#### Exam Line

Hamlet delays revenge due to religious and moral reasoning.

---

## 7️⃣ Seventh Soliloquy

📍 Act 4, Scene 4

Opening line:

> “How all occasions do inform against me”

---

#### 🧠 Meaning

Hamlet sees Fortinbras’s army ⚔️

Fortinbras fights bravely.

Hamlet feels ashamed.

He decides:

Now he will act.

---

#### 🎯 Keywords

Determination • Shame • Action • Revenge decision

---

#### Symbol

**Fortinbras = man of action**

---

#### Exam Line

Hamlet finally resolves to take revenge.

---

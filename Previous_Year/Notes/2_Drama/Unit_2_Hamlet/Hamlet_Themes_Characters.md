# 🟣 HAMLET

*Hamlet* is a tragedy by William Shakespeare set in Denmark.

The play begins when the Ghost of King Hamlet appears to soldiers and later to Prince Hamlet. The Ghost reveals that he was murdered by his own brother, **Claudius**, who has now become king and married Hamlet’s mother, **Queen Gertrude**. The Ghost commands Hamlet to take revenge.

Hamlet is deeply disturbed. Instead of acting immediately, he begins to question everything — life, death, morality, revenge, and truth. To investigate Claudius’ guilt, Hamlet pretends to be mad. He stages a play (“The Mousetrap”) that reenacts the murder. Claudius reacts guiltily, confirming the crime.

However, Hamlet delays killing Claudius. When he finally has the chance, Claudius is praying, and Hamlet hesitates again. Soon after, Hamlet mistakenly kills **Polonius**, thinking he is Claudius hiding behind a curtain.

This leads to tragic consequences:

* **Ophelia**, Polonius’ daughter and Hamlet’s love, goes mad and drowns.
* **Laertes**, Polonius’ son, returns seeking revenge.
* Claudius plots Hamlet’s death.

In the final duel between Hamlet and Laertes:

* Laertes poisons Hamlet with a sword.
* Gertrude accidentally drinks poisoned wine meant for Hamlet.
* Laertes is wounded by his own poisoned sword.
* Hamlet finally kills Claudius before dying.

The royal family is destroyed. Prince **Fortinbras** of Norway arrives and takes control of Denmark.

The play ends in total tragedy — revenge leads to universal destruction.

---

# 🟣 Major Themes

* **Revenge and Delay** – The moral struggle of avenging wrongs.
* **Appearance vs Reality** – Nothing is what it seems.
* **Madness (Real & Feigned)** – Hamlet pretends; Ophelia truly collapses.
* **Corruption and Decay** – “Something is rotten in the state of Denmark.”
* **Death and Mortality** – Constant reflection on life’s fragility.
* **Action vs Inaction** – Thinking too much prevents action.
* **Fate vs Free Will** – Are events destined or chosen?

# 🟣 Important Characters (Detailed)

## 1️⃣ Hamlet

- **Appearance:** Young prince, dressed in black (mourning).
- **Qualities:** Intelligent, philosophical, sensitive, indecisive, ironic.
- **Symbolism:** Represents the modern thinking individual — doubt and self-awareness.
- **Importance:** Central tragic hero. His hesitation causes destruction.
- He struggles between morality and revenge.

---

## 2️⃣ King Claudius

- **Appearance:** Royal, confident, politically smooth.
- **Qualities:** Ambitious, manipulative, guilty yet practical.
- **Symbolism:** Corruption and political evil.
- **Importance:** Main antagonist. Murderer of King Hamlet.

---

## 3️⃣ Queen Gertrude

- **Appearance:** Graceful queen.
- **Qualities:** Emotional, weak, possibly naive.
- **Symbolism:** Moral weakness and human frailty.
- **Importance:** Her quick remarriage disturbs Hamlet deeply.

---

## 4️⃣ Ophelia

- **Appearance:** Gentle, innocent young woman.
- **Qualities:** Obedient, pure, emotionally fragile.
- **Symbolism:** Female innocence destroyed by patriarchy and politics.
- **Importance:** Her madness contrasts Hamlet’s controlled madness.

---

## 5️⃣ Polonius

- **Appearance:** Elderly court advisor.
- **Qualities:** Talkative, spying, manipulative but foolish.
- **Symbolism:** Political interference and superficial wisdom.
- **Importance:** His death triggers tragedy.

---

## 6️⃣ Laertes

- **Appearance:** Young nobleman.
- **Qualities:** Passionate, impulsive, action-oriented.
- **Symbolism:** Man of action (contrast to Hamlet).
- **Importance:** Shows what Hamlet could have been without hesitation.

---

## 7️⃣ The Ghost

- **Appearance:** Armored spirit of dead king.
- **Qualities:** Commanding, mysterious.
- **Symbolism:** Revenge, unresolved past, moral justice.
- **Importance:** Starts the tragic chain of events.

---

## 8️⃣ Fortinbras

- **Qualities:** Brave, decisive.
- **Symbolism:** Political order and restoration.
- **Importance:** Ends the chaos and restores stability.

# 🟣 Most Important Quotes + Speaker + Explanation

### 1️⃣ “To be, or not to be: that is the question.”

**Speaker:** Hamlet (Act 3, Scene 1)
Hamlet questions whether it is nobler to live and suffer or to end one’s life. It reflects existential doubt, fear of the unknown after death, and his inner conflict about action.

---

### 2️⃣ “Something is rotten in the state of Denmark.”

**Speaker:** Marcellus (Act 1, Scene 4)
He says this after seeing the Ghost. The line symbolizes political corruption and moral decay caused by Claudius’ crime.

---

### 3️⃣ “Frailty, thy name is woman!”

**Speaker:** Hamlet (Act 1, Scene 2)
Hamlet expresses anger toward his mother for marrying Claudius so quickly. It reflects his disappointment and general frustration with human weakness.

---

### 4️⃣ “The lady doth protest too much, methinks.”

**Speaker:** Queen Gertrude (Act 3, Scene 2)
She comments on the queen in the play-within-the-play who promises eternal loyalty. The line highlights irony and the theme of appearance vs reality.

---

### 5️⃣ “Alas, poor Yorick! I knew him, Horatio.”

**Speaker:** Hamlet (Act 5, Scene 1)
Spoken in the graveyard while holding Yorick’s skull. It reflects on mortality and the inevitability of death for all — kings and commoners alike.

---

### 6️⃣ “The rest is silence.”

**Speaker:** Hamlet (Act 5, Scene 2)
His final words before dying. It symbolizes acceptance of death and the end of his suffering.

---

## 🟣 Important Soliloquies (With Speaker)

A **soliloquy** is a speech delivered by a character alone on stage, revealing their inner thoughts and feelings directly to the audience.

### 1️⃣ “To be or not to be”

**Speaker:** Hamlet
Theme: Life vs death, existence, suffering.

---

### 2️⃣ “O that this too too solid flesh would melt”

**Speaker:** Hamlet (Act 1, Scene 2)
Theme: Grief, disgust at his mother’s remarriage, desire for escape from pain.

---

### 3️⃣ “What a rogue and peasant slave am I!”

**Speaker:** Hamlet (Act 2, Scene 2)
Theme: Self-criticism for delaying revenge; realization that he must act.

## 🟣 Why Hamlet is Important

* Most psychologically complex Shakespeare play.
* Introduces modern existential thought.
* Defines the “tragic hero with inner conflict.”
* Influenced philosophy, literature, and drama for centuries.

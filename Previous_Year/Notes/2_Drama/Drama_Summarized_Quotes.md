# Drama Summarized and Main Quotes

# 🎭 UNIT I : *Dr. Faustus* (Christopher Marlowe)

## 🔹 Start of the Play

Faustus rejects traditional studies (theology, law, medicine) and decides to practice **magic**.

## 🔹 End of the Play

Faustus repents too late and is **dragged to hell** by devils.

## 👤 Main Characters (Catch Points)

* **Faustus** – ambitious scholar, tragic overreacher
* **Mephistopheles** – devil, intelligent and ironic
* **Good Angel / Evil Angel** – inner moral conflict

## 🎯 Major Themes

* Ambition and overreaching
* Knowledge vs religion
* Damnation and repentance
* Free will

## 📌 3 MOST IMPORTANT QUOTES

1. **“A sound magician is a mighty god.”**
   → Faustus’ pride and ambition
2. **“Why, this is hell, nor am I out of it.”**
   → Psychological hell, not just physical
3. **“I’ll burn my books.”**
   → Realisation comes too late

# 🎭 UNIT II : *The Tempest* (William Shakespeare)

## 🔹 Start

A magical storm (tempest) shipwrecks Prospero’s enemies on an island.

## 🔹 End

Prospero forgives everyone, regains his dukedom, and **renounces magic**.

## 👤 Characters

* **Prospero** – magician, rightful duke
* **Miranda** – innocence and purity
* **Caliban** – colonised native figure
* **Ariel** – spirit of art and freedom

## 🎯 Themes

* Forgiveness vs revenge
* Power and authority
* Art and illusion
* Colonisation

## 📌 3 IMPORTANT QUOTES

1. **“We are such stuff as dreams are made on.”**
   → Life as illusion
2. **“The rarer action is / In virtue than in vengeance.”**
   → Moral centre of the play
3. **“This thing of darkness I acknowledge mine.”**
   → Responsibility and guilt

# 🎭 UNIT III : *She Stoops to Conquer* (Oliver Goldsmith)

## 🔹 Start

A practical joke leads visitors to mistake a rich house for an inn.

## 🔹 End

Misunderstandings clear, marriages are approved, **comic harmony restored**.

## 👤 Characters

* **Kate Hardcastle** – smart, adaptable heroine
* **Marlow** – shy gentleman
* **Tony Lumpkin** – comic mischief-maker

## 🎯 Themes

* Appearance vs reality
* Natural behaviour
* Class and manners
* Marriage

## 📌 3 IMPORTANT QUOTES

1. **“Confidence is the result of our own opinion of ourselves.”**
2. **“I laugh and that is enough for me.”**
3. **“Ask me no questions, and I’ll tell you no lies.”**

---

# 🎭 UNIT IV : *A Doll’s House* (Henrik Ibsen)

## 🔹 Start

A seemingly happy middle-class marriage.

## 🔹 End

Nora **leaves her husband and children** to find her identity (famous door slam).

## 👤 Characters

* **Nora** – evolving, self-realising woman
* **Torvald** – controlling husband
* **Krogstad** – moral mirror

## 🎯 Themes

* Feminism
* Identity
* Marriage and patriarchy
* Individual freedom

## 📌 3 IMPORTANT QUOTES

1. **“I believe that before all else I am a human being.”**
2. **“I have been your doll-wife.”**
3. **“The most wonderful thing of all would have to happen.”**

---

# 🎭 UNIT V : *The Zoo Story* (Edward Albee)

## 🔹 Start

Two strangers meet casually on a park bench.

## 🔹 End

Jerry provokes his own death → **violent communication**.

## 👤 Characters

* **Peter** – passive, middle-class comfort
* **Jerry** – alienated, aggressive outsider

## 🎯 Themes

* Alienation
* Breakdown of communication
* Absurdity of modern life
* Loneliness

## 📌 3 IMPORTANT QUOTES

1. **“Sometimes it’s necessary to go a long distance out of the way.”**
2. **“I came unto you at the zoo.”**
3. **“You’re not really a nice person.”**

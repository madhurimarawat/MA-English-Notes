# 📘 1. DOCTOR FAUSTUS – Christopher Marlowe

## 🔹 Summary

*Doctor Faustus* tells the story of a brilliant scholar who becomes dissatisfied with traditional knowledge and makes a pact with the devil. Faustus sells his soul to Lucifer for 24 years of unlimited power and knowledge, served by Mephistopheles. Instead of using this power for noble purposes, he wastes it on tricks, entertainment, and sensual pleasures. As the end approaches, Faustus struggles with repentance but cannot fully turn back to God.

## 🔺 Climax

The final scene, when the clock strikes midnight and devils drag Faustus to hell. His desperate cries show ultimate damnation.

## 🔹 Themes

* Ambition and overreaching pride
* Knowledge vs wisdom
* Sin, repentance, and damnation
* Free will vs fate
* Renaissance humanism

## 🔹 Main Characters

* **Faustus** – Ambitious scholar, tragic overreacher
* **Mephistopheles** – Devil, tempter, tragic awareness
* **Lucifer** – Embodiment of evil
* **Good Angel / Evil Angel** – Moral conflict
* **Helen of Troy** – Illusion, sensual beauty

# 📘 2. HAMLET – William Shakespeare

## 🔹 Summary

Prince Hamlet learns from his father’s ghost that King Claudius murdered him. Hamlet pretends madness to expose the truth but delays revenge due to moral doubt. His hesitation leads to accidental deaths, madness, and betrayal. In the end, the royal family is destroyed.

## 🔺 Climax

The duel scene where Gertrude, Laertes, Claudius, and Hamlet all die.

## 🔹 Themes

* Revenge and delay
* Appearance vs reality
* Madness (real and feigned)
* Corruption and decay
* Death and mortality

## 🔹 Main Characters

* **Hamlet** – Tragic hero, thinker
* **Claudius** – Usurping king
* **Gertrude** – Morally weak queen
* **Ophelia** – Innocent victim
* **Laertes** – Man of action
* **Ghost** – Revenge and justice

# 📘 3. THE WAY OF THE WORLD – William Congreve

## 🔹 Summary

A comedy of manners centered on Mirabell and Millamant, who wish to marry without losing independence or inheritance. Lady Wishfort’s consent is required, leading to deception, wit, and schemes. Ultimately, truth triumphs, and the lovers are united.

## 🔺 Climax

The exposure of Fainall’s plot and Lady Wishfort’s consent to the marriage.

## 🔹 Themes

* Marriage and money
* Wit and social hypocrisy
* Love vs convenience
* Gender roles
* Morality behind manners

## 🔹 Main Characters

* **Mirabell** – Intelligent, controlled hero
* **Millamant** – Independent, witty heroine
* **Lady Wishfort** – Vain, comic figure
* **Fainall** – Villainous opportunist
* **Mrs. Marwood** – Scheming antagonist

# 📘 4. SAINT JOAN – George Bernard Shaw

## 🔹 Summary

Based on the life of Joan of Arc, the play shows her rise as a divinely inspired leader and her fall through political and religious opposition. Though victorious in war, she is tried by the Church and executed. Later, she is declared innocent.

## 🔺 Climax

Joan’s execution at the stake.

## 🔹 Themes

* Individual vs authority
* Faith vs institutional religion
* Nationalism
* Feminism
* Martyrdom

## 🔹 Main Characters

* **Joan** – Visionary, courageous heroine
* **Bishop Cauchon** – Church authority
* **Charles VII** – Weak king
* **Warwick** – Political power
* **Dunois** – Practical supporter

# 📘 5. THE ZOO STORY – Edward Albee

## 🔹 Summary

A one-act play about two men, Peter and Jerry, who meet on a park bench. Jerry desperately seeks human connection, while Peter lives a comfortable but empty life. Their conversation escalates into violence, ending in Jerry’s death.

## 🔺 Climax

Jerry forces Peter to stab him with a knife.

## 🔹 Themes

* Alienation and loneliness
* Failure of communication
* Class conflict
* Violence as connection
* Absurdity of modern life

## 🔹 Main Characters

* **Jerry** – Isolated, desperate, tragic
* **Peter** – Comfortable, passive, disconnected

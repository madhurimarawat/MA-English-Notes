# 🏰 1️⃣ Morality (Medieval Concept) in *Faustus*

This comes from the **Middle Ages** mindset.

### 💡 Main Idea:

Life is about:

* God
* Salvation
* Heaven & Hell
* Obedience to religion

In medieval morality plays:

* There is a clear message: **Sin → Punishment**
* Good vs Evil is very clear.
* Repentance saves you.

### 📌 In *Faustus*:

* The Good Angel and Evil Angel represent moral struggle.
* Faustus is repeatedly told to repent.
* He refuses.
* In the end → He is dragged to Hell.

👉 So the play teaches a clear moral lesson:
**Over-ambition + rejecting God = Damnation**

That’s very medieval thinking.

---

# 🌟 2️⃣ Renaissance Concept in *Faustus*

Now comes the exciting part 😌

The Renaissance (rebirth period) believed in:

* Human potential
* Individual ambition
* Knowledge & science
* Questioning religion
* “Man can achieve anything”

Faustus represents this spirit.

### 💡 How?

He says normal subjects are boring:

* Logic
* Medicine
* Law
* Theology

He wants:

* Unlimited knowledge
* Magical power
* To control nature
* To become “like a god”

This is pure Renaissance ambition.

He is not satisfied with limits.
He wants **infinite power**.

---

# ⚡ So What Is Marlowe Showing?

Faustus is:

✨ A Renaissance man
BUT
⚖️ Trapped in a medieval moral universe

He has modern ambition
But the world still runs on religious morality.

And that’s the tragedy.

---

# 🧠 Easy Exam Line

*Doctor Faustus presents the conflict between Renaissance human ambition and medieval Christian morality, where Faustus’ desire for unlimited knowledge leads to his tragic damnation.*

# Writers Era, Age, Titles, Admirers, Critics, Contribution

# 🟣 CHRISTOPHER MARLOWE

### 📅 Era & Age

* Elizabethan Age
* 16th Century
* Contemporary of Shakespeare

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1564**, Canterbury, England
* Educated at Cambridge University
* Allegedly involved in espionage
* Died: **1593**, stabbed in a tavern fight (mysterious death) at age 29

### 📚 Famous Works

* *Doctor Faustus*
* *Tamburlaine the Great*
* *The Jew of Malta*
* *Edward II*

### 🏆 Titles & Who Gave Them

* **“Father of English Tragedy”** – literary critics
* **“Morning Star of English Drama”** – critics (because he prepared the way for Shakespeare)

### 👏 Admirers

* Influenced Shakespeare
* Goethe admired *Doctor Faustus*

### ⚖️ Critics

* Accused of atheism
* Criticized for violent and rebellious heroes

### 🌟 Contribution

* Perfected blank verse
* Introduced the “overreacher” tragic hero
* Brought grandeur to English drama

---

# 🟣 WILLIAM SHAKESPEARE

### 📅 Era & Age

* Elizabethan & Jacobean Age
* 1564–1616

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1564**, Stratford-upon-Avon
* Married Anne Hathaway
* Actor, playwright, shareholder of Globe Theatre
* Died: **1616**, Stratford

### 📚 Famous Works

* *Hamlet, Macbeth, King Lear, Othello*
* *As You Like It, Twelfth Night*
* *The Tempest*

### 🏆 Titles & Who Gave Them

* **“Bard of Avon”** – popular traditional title
* **Ben Jonson** called him “Soul of the Age”
* Known as **National Poet of England**

### 👏 Admirers

* Ben Jonson
* Coleridge
* Charles Lamb

### ⚖️ Critics

* Voltaire criticized his irregular plots
* Dr. Johnson criticized lack of classical unity

### 🌟 Contribution

* Master of tragedy & comedy
* Psychological characterization
* Enriched English language
* Universal themes

---

# 🟣 OLIVER GOLDSMITH

### 📅 Era & Age

* 18th Century
* Neoclassical / Age of Johnson

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1728**, Ireland
* Struggled financially
* Friend of Dr. Samuel Johnson
* Died: **1774**, London

### 📚 Famous Works

* *The Vicar of Wakefield*
* *She Stoops to Conquer*
* *The Deserted Village*

### 🏆 Titles & Who Gave Them

* Known as **“Poet of Rural Life”** – literary critics
* Member of Johnson’s Literary Club

### 👏 Admirers

* Dr. Johnson
* Edmund Burke

### ⚖️ Critics

* Criticized for sentimentality
* Some called his style too simple

### 🌟 Contribution

* Revived sentimental comedy
* Blended humor with moral lessons
* Depicted village life beautifully

---

# 🟣 HENRIK IBSEN

### 📅 Era & Age

* 19th Century
* Modern Age
* Norwegian dramatist

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1828**, Norway
* Lived in Italy & Germany for many years
* Died: **1906**, Oslo

### 📚 Famous Works

* *A Doll’s House*
* *Ghosts*
* *Hedda Gabler*
* *The Wild Duck*

### 🏆 Titles & Who Gave Them

* **“Father of Modern Drama”** – literary critics
* **“Father of Realism”** – literary historians

### 👏 Admirers

* George Bernard Shaw
* James Joyce

### ⚖️ Critics

* Criticized for attacking social institutions
* *Ghosts* called immoral by early critics

### 🌟 Contribution

* Introduced realism in drama
* Social problem plays
* Psychological depth
* Feminist themes

---

# 🟣 EDWARD ALBEE

### 📅 Era & Age

* 20th Century
* Modern / Postmodern American Drama
* Theatre of the Absurd

### 👤 Life & Death

* Born: **1928**, USA
* Adopted by wealthy family
* Won multiple Pulitzer Prizes
* Died: **2016**, New York

### 📚 Famous Works

* *Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf?*
* *The Zoo Story*
* *The American Dream*

### 🏆 Titles & Who Gave Them

* Major figure of **Theatre of the Absurd** – critics’ classification
* Pulitzer Prize Winner – officially awarded

### 👏 Admirers

* Influenced by Samuel Beckett
* Respected in American theatre

### ⚖️ Critics

* Criticized for harsh realism
* Some found his plays disturbing

### 🌟 Contribution

* Brought absurdism to America
* Explored illusion vs reality
* Deep psychological conflicts
